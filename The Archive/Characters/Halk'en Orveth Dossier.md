## II. WHAT HE IS

Halk'en Orveth is one of the seven living Rector-Ascendants recognized within restricted ecclesiastical doctrine as an Answered-class Crowned vessel.

This must be distinguished from ordinary Beatification and from lesser Magnitudes of Crowned status.

Most Divine Vessels processed into Obsidian carry angelic Presence at lesser Magnitudes. A Votary may bear light-domain Presence, protection-domain Presence, healing-domain Presence, or some rarer expression through which the Sovereign's authority becomes operationally useful in the field. In Obsidian's own formal language, the stable and non-erosive version of this condition is sanctified Bearing: transformation rather than possession, integration rather than displacement. This is already dangerous. It is already transformative. It already requires formation, confession, surgical monitoring, and the permanent acceptance that the vessel will not remain entirely what they were before investiture.

The Answered are not that.

The Answered carry contact with the Seraphic **Cynosure** from which the rest of a constellation proceeds. They do not contain the Seraphim in full. No mortal recursive singularity could. What they carry is central-star Weight expressed through Presence at unusual depth: a continuous and stabilized Divinatory alignment deep enough that subordinate angelic offices within that constellation may express through them without requiring separate vessel contact.

In Halk'en Orveth's case, the central star expressed through that fusion is **Auctoriel, Seraphim of Dominion**.

The Church teaches Dominion as the holy principle by which rightful authority becomes operative in the world. Dominion is not domination, though every generation of clergy must be taught the difference again. It is not mere command. It is the act by which command becomes binding, by which office becomes burden, by which sentence becomes consequence, by which the hand laid upon a head, shoulder, weapon, wound, oath, or throat makes visible that authority has ceased to be abstract.

This is why Father Orveth's manifestation is not a crown, a voice, a flame, or a winged apparition.

It is hands.

Seven of them.

The hands do not symbolize Dominion. They are Dominion's contact-state, expressed through a mortal vessel whose priesthood, knighthood, and four centuries of service have made him an acceptable surface for Auctoriel's pressure.

He remains human.

This is still true.

It is not always reassuring.

## III. APPEARANCE, TEMPERAMENT, AND SOCIAL PRESENCE

Halk'en Orveth appears to be a man in late middle age, though this appearance has remained broadly stable for more than two centuries. The preservation is not Redactory self-modification in the Archtype sense and not biological youthfulness in the saintly sense. His body has aged into a shape his fusion maintains because that shape has become part of his office: old enough to be obeyed, strong enough to act, worn enough not to mistake strength for innocence.

He is approximately 190 centimeters tall, broad through the shoulders, and heavily built even outside armor. His frame has the density of a man who wore plate before his body finished deciding what it was. His face is severe without being sharp. The nose has been broken at least twice. The left cheek carries a long pale scar from a pre-investiture field injury sustained during the Mornhallow possession riots. His beard is close-trimmed, black gone mostly iron-grey. His eyes are dark brown under ordinary conditions and turn a deep red-gold when Auctoriel expresses through him at fourth-hand intensity or higher.

In clerical dress, he favors plain black cassock, iron rosary, and no visible ornament except the red cord of Rector-Ascendant authority knotted at the left wrist. He dislikes ceremonial excess. This dislike is not humility. It is functional theology. To Father Orveth, office is already heavy. Adornment that does not increase the bearer's capacity to carry office is, at best, irrelevant.

In armor, he becomes difficult to look away from.

His field plate is blackened steel of pre-modern form, maintained through Obsidian artificers and adjusted across centuries rather than replaced. It is not modern military equipment and does not attempt to be. The silhouette is medieval: high gorget, layered pauldrons, reinforced breast, articulated gauntlets, sabatons, and a narrow visor helm whose faceplate gives only a vertical slit of visibility. Red enamel fills the incised lines across the backplate, where seven palm-marks radiate from the spine like an inverted halo. When the Sevenfold Hand manifests, the hands emerge through those marks as if the armor has been built less to contain him than to provide the correct doors.

His ordinary physical manner is restrained. He does not touch casually. This is not coldness. It is discipline. He asks permission before laying a hand on a penitent outside sacramental requirement. He removes his gauntlets before blessing children. He does not shake hands with civilians unless they offer first, and even then he does so with a care that younger clergy sometimes mistake for awkwardness.

He is not awkward.

He knows what touch means.

In conversation, he is direct, formal, and patient to a degree that unsettles people who expected a battlefield saint to be more forceful. He speaks slowly, not because he lacks speed of thought, but because he dislikes wasting command on sentences that do not need it. He almost never raises his voice. Witnesses of his field actions repeatedly note that the moment before violence is often marked by his voice becoming softer.

He is kind in a way that does not comfort everyone.

He is gentle without being permissive.

He forgives slowly, but when he does, the forgiveness has weight.

## IV. AUCTORIEL, SERAPHIM OF DOMINION

### IV.1 Auctoriel as Cynosure

Auctoriel is one of the **Seven Answers**; Orveth is one of the **Answered** recognized in the restricted doctrine of the Interior Council.

Public Church teaching does not enumerate the Seven Answers, and it does not publicly name the Answered as a mortal category. Sermons speak of the Sovereign's high attendants, the burning offices of Heaven, and the great powers by which His will is expressed. The full Seraphic taxonomy is not taught to ordinary clergy. It is held within the Interior Council's restricted theology because the difference between worship, fragment contact, and uncontrolled Seraphic fascination has killed enough people to justify silence.

Auctoriel's domain is Dominion.

The term is often misunderstood. In civic speech, dominion suggests rulership, ownership, conquest, or possession. In restricted theology, Auctoriel does not govern power as appetite. Auctoriel governs the sacred condition under which authority becomes binding.

Dominion is the principle behind:

- ordination;
- investiture;
- lawful command;
- oath-taking;
- rightful restraint;
- correction;
- sentence;
- jurisdiction;
- the laying-on of hands;
- the taking-up of office;
- the placing of burden;
- the command by which a possessing entity is told it has no right to remain.

Auctoriel is not mercy.

Auctoriel is not justice in full.

Auctoriel is not obedience considered as virtue.

Auctoriel is the terrible hinge between command and reality: the instant at which a word, office, oath, blessing, verdict, or prohibition stops being an intention and becomes something the world must answer.

### IV.2 The Sevenfold Expression

The Seraphim's pressure expresses through Father Orveth as seven distinguishable hands. These hands are not separate angels in the ordinary fragment taxonomy, though lesser angelic offices of Dominion may be understood through them. They are contact-aspects of a single Seraphic Cynosure and its constellation.

Their visibility correlates with strength, conceptual depth, and operational authority. Lower expressions may be present without ordinary witnesses seeing them. Higher expressions are visible, tangible, and measurable by every instrument Obsidian has yet placed near them.

The Ecclesiastic Surgeons use the term **hand-count** as shorthand for active expression level. This is useful in field records and inadequate in theology.

One hand is not merely weaker than three.

One hand is a different kind of authority.

## V. THE SEVENFOLD HAND

### V.1 First Hand: The Unseen Hand - Permission

The first hand is almost never seen. It is present more often than Father Orveth acknowledges in conversation and more often than field observers can verify.

When visible under specialist observation, it appears as a faint red distortion near his right shoulder blade: smaller than an adult hand, not fully formed, with fingers suggested by pressure rather than outline. It does not grasp. It does not strike. It hovers.

Its domain is **Permission**.

This is the hand by which Father Orveth's authority finds lawful entrance into a situation. It does not overpower. It determines whether there is a place for command to stand.

Effects associated with the Unseen Hand include:

- recognition of valid spiritual jurisdiction;
- resistance to demonic attempts to declare a space outside Church address;
- detection of false authority or forged consecration;
- stabilization of the first phrase of the Naming;
- refusal of hostile invitation rites;
- quiet prevention of command spoken without right.

Most witnesses never know it has acted. They only observe that a room which should have resisted intervention becomes available to it.

The Unseen Hand is the reason Father Orveth is difficult to bar from a possession site once he has been properly sent.

### V.2 Second Hand: The Veiled Hand - Witness

The second hand is also rarely visible to ordinary sight. In reflective surfaces, under high-Dive scrutiny, or near oath-bound vessels, it appears as a slender, feminine hand of translucent red, long-fingered and still. Several witnesses have described it as beautiful. Those same witnesses usually retract the word after considering what it did.

Its domain is **Witness**.

The Veiled Hand records the condition under which command becomes answerable. It does not judge in the punitive sense. It makes evasion harder by establishing that something has been seen, heard, sworn, confessed, or named.

Effects associated with the Veiled Hand include:

- pressure against lying during formal exorcistic inquiry;
- preservation of true names once spoken;
- detection of concealed oaths, bargains, and demonic claims;
- stabilizing testimony from frightened or partially possessed witnesses;
- preventing a possessing entity from hiding behind the vessel's own memories;
- forcing a broken confession to complete enough for sacramental action.

Father Orveth is cautious with this hand. He considers witness to be one of the most easily abused forms of holy power. The record supports this caution.

### V.3 Third Hand: The Grasping Hand - Restraint

The third hand is the first expression most people see.

It is large, rough, and visibly unlike the second. The palm is broad. The fingers are thick, scarred, and uneven. The knuckles appear almost armored. The red opacity remains ghostly at low expression, but the outline is clear enough that even untrained civilians can identify it once manifestation stabilizes.

Its domain is **Restraint**.

The Grasping Hand is the hand most associated with Father Orveth's battlefield reputation. It seizes, pins, drags, catches, blocks, and holds. It can restrain a possessed vessel with enough care not to shatter the body. It can catch weapons. It can hold doors against pressure that has already broken physical locks. It can close around a demonic manifestation's throat and prevent speech long enough for the Naming to land.

Effects associated with the Grasping Hand include:

- physical restraint of hostile vessels;
- disruption of demonic motor control;
- catching or redirecting weapons within short reach;
- anchoring Father Orveth's own body under impact;
- preventing retreat from consecrated containment;
- holding open or closed a threshold under structural pressure.

The Grasping Hand is the one most soldiers remember.

It is not the one demons fear most.

### V.4 Fourth Hand: The Open Hand - Investiture

The fourth hand is visibly more refined. It appears as an open palm, red and luminous, with a steady outline and a quality of stillness that field reports often describe as ceremonial. When it manifests, the air near Father Orveth becomes difficult to interrupt. People lower their voices without being told.

Its domain is **Investiture**.

This is the hand by which burden, office, or temporary authority is placed upon another. It can lay courage over terror, endurance over collapse, exorcistic steadiness over a failing priest, or command-capacity over a subordinate for the duration of a rite. It does not create ability where no structure exists. It places weight where the recipient can, briefly, bear it.

Effects associated with the Open Hand include:

- temporary reinforcement of priestly authority during exorcism;
- enabling junior Votaries to maintain barriers above ordinary tolerance;
- stabilizing a witness long enough to speak;
- placing a mission-burden upon a selected subordinate;
- allowing a civilian to carry or touch a consecrated object without immediate collapse;
- transferring responsibility in a manner recognized by Auctoriel's pressure.

This hand has produced some of Father Orveth's greatest saves and some of his most controversial field decisions. A person can survive being made greater for ten seconds and spend the rest of their life injured by the memory.

### V.5 Fifth Hand: The Accusing Hand - Judgment

The fifth hand is clearly visible to all witnesses and significantly denser than the first four. It is long, angular, and sharp-edged in outline, with a prominent index finger and a palm that rarely opens fully. Its red color deepens toward dark arterial light. In several cases, mirrored surfaces have cracked when the Accusing Hand pointed toward them during possession inquiry.

Its domain is **Judgment**.

The Accusing Hand does not deliver final sentence. It establishes that judgment has become unavoidable. It points toward the part of a person, demon, institution, oath, or structure that must answer.

Effects associated with the Accusing Hand include:

- forcing possessing entities to answer under the correct category;
- compelling recognition of guilt, false claim, or unlawful presence;
- making hidden spiritual ownership visible to trained observers;
- preventing a demon from dispersing through multiple vessel-voices;
- marking the object, body, or rite-state against which Sundersaint may act most cleanly;
- gathering room-level attention toward the morally decisive point of the encounter.

Father Orveth does not raise the fifth hand lightly.

When he does, even allied clergy often feel accused.

This may be unavoidable.

It may also be the first sign of Dominion becoming pastorally dangerous.

### V.6 Sixth Hand: The Hand of Mandate - Command

The sixth hand is fully tangible.

This is not a poetic description. Every measurement method Obsidian has approved for divine manifestation has registered the sixth hand as present in a way lower expressions are not. It has measurable resistance, pressure, temperature variance, mass-like interaction, and structural density sufficient to interfere with Redactory readings, BrightCrystal instrumentation, acoustic mapping, and ordinary physical contact.

It appears as a large right hand of deep opaque red, neither rough like the third nor elegant like the second. It is exact. The proportions are human enough to be intelligible and wrong enough to be distressing. When it closes around something, witnesses often report the impression that the closing happened first and the movement followed.

Its domain is **Command**.

The Hand of Mandate imposes a direct binding instruction upon the target, provided Father Orveth possesses lawful authority to speak that instruction in the relevant context. It cannot command arbitrary impossibility. It cannot make the dead live, make the innocent guilty, or cause a structure to perform what it has no coherent capacity to perform. It can, however, force an already-contested structure to answer the command it has been evading.

Effects associated with the Hand of Mandate include:

- stopping a demonic manifestation mid-action;
- forcing a possessing entity to release a body part, voice, memory, or threshold;
- halting a violent vessel without destroying the vessel;
- compelling a hostile rite-state to stabilize long enough to be cut, named, or expelled;
- overriding lesser angelic or demonic Weight in direct contest;
- making an oathbreaker experience the structural weight of the violated oath.

This is the point at which Father Orveth ceases to resemble even an unusually powerful Rector-Ascendant.

The sixth hand is why the Keepers of Before classify him as a strategic instrument rather than merely a cleric.

### V.7 Seventh Hand: The Sovereign Palm - Dominion

The seventh hand has appeared in confirmed record twelve times.

Unconfirmed appearances raise the probable number to seventeen.

It is not the largest hand. This fact recurs in every reliable witness account. The expectation, among those foolish enough to have one, is that the seventh expression of Dominion would be vast. It is not. It appears behind Father Orveth, slightly above the spine, palm outward, full and perfectly formed. The color is deep red with no translucence at all. It casts no light and yet is visible in darkness. It does not flicker. It does not move unless it must.

Its domain is **Dominion**.

Not permission, witness, restraint, investiture, judgment, or command. Dominion itself.

The Sovereign Palm does not merely command a target. It imposes the condition that the target is under authority. The distinction is enormous. A demon resisting expulsion may ignore words, twist ritual sequence, fracture vessel memory, or exploit jurisdictional uncertainty. Under the Sovereign Palm, the question of jurisdiction closes. The target becomes answerable.

Effects associated with the Sovereign Palm include:

- forcing fragment-class demonic presences into answerable state;
- collapsing false claims of ownership over a vessel;
- imposing temporary hierarchical order over chaotic multi-vessel possession events;
- allowing Sundersaint to sever bindings that would otherwise resist contact;
- making hostile divine, demonic, or anomalous manifestations present themselves as something that can be addressed;
- transforming a room-level disaster into a court.

This hand carries unacceptable risk.

Every confirmed manifestation has been followed by no fewer than thirty days of Ecclesiastic Surgical assessment, seven consecutive confessions, and Interior Council review. The concern is not that Father Orveth will become demonic. That category is not useful here. The concern is that a mortal vessel who expresses Dominion at seventh-hand depth may eventually stop experiencing his own restraint as separate from Auctoriel's will.

Father Orveth himself has written only one sentence on the matter:

> *"The seventh hand is not mine, and therefore I must answer for it more carefully than if it were."*

The Interior Council considers this a healthy statement.

It is also the kind of statement a man makes when he knows the edge is real.

## VI. SUNDERSAINT, THE PARTING MERCY

### VI.1 Identity of the Artifact

Father Orveth bears the greater artifact sword known in common Obsidian usage as **Sundersaint**.

Its formal restricted designation is:

> **Sundersaint, the Parting Mercy**

The weapon predates the Sundering of Selia by approximately one century. It was forged in the late pre-Imperial period by a divine artificer whose surviving name appears in three incompatible forms: **Iovren Sahl**, **Jo'vren Sael**, and **Iovran of the Cleaving Hymn**. The most reliable Church record identifies him as a Divinator aligned to an angelic fragment of **Severance**, not to Auctoriel and not to any known Seraphic constellation presently associated with the Seven Answers.

The distinction matters.

Sundersaint is not an extension of Auctoriel. It does not express Dominion. It expresses Severance through BrightCrystal-based weaponry, and it remains connected to the original divine fragment that answered its forging.

The sword's current cooperation with Father Orveth is therefore not automatic. It is a relationship between two divine principles: Dominion and Severance. Father Orveth commands. Sundersaint parts.

When the relationship functions correctly, the result is among the most dangerous exorcistic instruments in the Church's possession.

### VI.2 Physical Description

Sundersaint is a longsword of unusual brightness and unusual restraint.

The blade is BrightCrystal-derived, though the material has been stabilized into a milky translucent state rather than the clear or radiant forms common in industrial refinement. Fine red-gold veins run through the crystal in irregular lines, visible only when the blade catches Moonlight or divine manifestation. The edge is not metallic. It is a maintained boundary-condition within the crystal itself, sharpened by the artifact's ongoing Severance alignment rather than by conventional grinding.

The crossguard is plain black iron, later replacement, third Imperial century. The grip is dark leather over a narrow relic core. The pommel contains a sealed shard believed to be from the original forging matrix. No attempt to open it has been authorized since 1432, when the last examination caused three assisting priests to forget the final hour of the procedure and one Redactor to lose all sensation in his left hand for nine years.

The sword is not beautiful in the ornamental sense.

It is beautiful in the way a sentence can be beautiful when there is no word in it that can be removed.

### VI.3 Divine Function

Sundersaint's concept is **Severance**.

Whatever the blade touches becomes inclined toward separation according to the nearest coherent fault-line. This is not omnipotent cutting. The sword does not simply divide anything the wielder wishes. It finds what is already separable, contested, falsely joined, parasitically attached, oath-bound beyond right, or structurally ready to part, and applies divine pressure toward division.

Known effects include:

- weakening demonic attachment to a vessel;
- cutting through possession bindings without necessarily killing the host;
- separating a hostile manifestation from its borrowed physical medium;
- severing false oaths, parasitic covenants, and imposed compulsions;
- causing weapons, armor, or barriers to part along existing flaws;
- making a lie lose coherence when touched through written, spoken, or symbolic medium;
- separating a rite-state from the object sustaining it.

Sundersaint does not decide what should be severed.

That is why it is bearable.

A sword that both judged and severed would be an atrocity waiting for a hand.

Father Orveth supplies judgment through Dominion. This is effective. It is also why the pairing is watched.

### VI.4 Limits

Sundersaint cannot sever what has no coherent separability. It cannot divide a soul from a living body without catastrophic theological and structural consequence, and Father Orveth has never attempted this in approved record. It cannot erase a demon. It cannot destroy an angelic fragment. It cannot cut Archive-valid information out of existence.

It also cannot replace exorcism.

The sword can open the place where expulsion becomes possible. It cannot perform contrition, confession, Naming, or restoration. Father Orveth has corrected younger Votaries on this point with unusual severity.

His most repeated instruction regarding the artifact is preserved in formation records:

> *"The sword parts. It does not save. Do not confuse a clean cut with mercy."*

## VII. EARLY LIFE

Halk'en Orveth was born in 1801 in the village-parish of Black Mere, a cold settlement in the Empire's Northern Region, positioned along a river road used by timber convoys, minor pilgrims, and military supply traffic bound toward the Threshold stations. The region's records are incomplete due to later flooding, but the baptismal ledger survives in copy. It names his parents as **Maren Orveth**, a ferryman and lay cantor, and **Selka Orveth**, a parish copyist.

Nothing in the surviving record indicates noble standing, Redactory lineage, or unusual political connection.

The apostrophe position in his first name is ordinary for the working population. This fact has been remarked upon by later commentators with more interest than it deserves. Halk'en Orveth did not come from a House. He did not need to. The Church has always preferred to remember this when it is useful and forget it when aristocratic donors are present.

His earliest recorded distinction was physical courage, though this is less illuminating than later hagiographic sketches suggest. Northern children in Black Mere learned early that cold water, bad roads, injured animals, and frightened adults did not wait for courage to become spiritually meaningful. At twelve, he pulled a younger child from a millrace during Low Radiance flood. At fourteen, he struck a possessed horse with a shovel until it stopped trampling a priest. At fifteen, he carried an injured priest through snow to Saint Deyr's hospice and collapsed with frostbite in three toes.

The priest survived.

The toes did not fully recover.

This left him with a slight stiffness in his right foot that persists even under Seraphic preservation. He has refused corrective intervention on three occasions.

At sixteen, he entered minor seminary.

The formation notes do not describe a prodigy. They describe a large, quiet boy with strong memory, poor tolerance for ornate theological debate, excellent liturgical discipline, and a habit of interpreting doctrine through physical obligation. He struggled with speculative metaphysics. He excelled in pastoral crisis, funerary rites, and the practical mechanics of exorcistic preparation.

A novice-master wrote in 1819:

> *"He does not ask what authority means. He asks who is carrying it and whether they are standing where they should."*

The sentence has been quoted too often in later profiles.

It remains accurate.

## VIII. ENTRY INTO THE OBSIDIAN RITE

### VIII.1 The Mornhallow Incident

The event that brought Halk'en Orveth to Obsidian occurred in 1823, during what later became known as the **Mornhallow possession riots**.

Mornhallow was a market town built around three bridges and a small ecclesiastical hospital. Over thirteen days, a demonic pressure event moved through the town's sickrooms, attaching first to fever patients, then to attendants, then to family members keeping vigil. The local clergy misclassified the event as sequential possession. It was not. Later review determined that the pressure had formed a distributed vessel-network across multiple weakened bodies, using grief as the connective tissue.

Orveth was twenty-two, recently ordained, and present only because he had been assigned to assist in flood-season funerary overflow.

The first Obsidian unit arrived too late and under strength. The senior Warden was killed within an hour. Two priests lost speech. The hospital's west ward became structurally contested and could not be entered without immediate vocal mimicry from the patients inside.

The surviving account from Sister Aveline Mor, then a junior Ecclesiastic Surgeon, records the decisive moment:

> *"Father Orveth walked to the ward doors after we told him not to. He placed his bare hand on the lintel, then on his own chest, then on the door. He did not perform the Naming. He said, 'This house was given to the sick. You were not given leave.' The voices stopped. Not forever. Long enough."*

The first red hand appeared then, though no one recognized it as Seraphic.

Sister Aveline described it only as a pressure behind him shaped like a hand that had not yet decided to be visible.

The ward was entered. Eleven patients survived who should not have.

Obsidian removed Orveth from ordinary parish assignment within the month.

### VIII.2 Early Warden Formation

Orveth entered Obsidian formation in 1824 under **Rector-Ascendant Mael Orison**, a light-domain field commander whose patience for unusual candidates was famous and not always wise. Orison's early assessments repeatedly note that Orveth did not respond like a candidate seeking power. He responded like a man irritated by power until someone told him where to put it.

He was poor at radiance projection. He was adequate in formal ward geometry. He was excellent in vessel restraint, contested-threshold entry, and post-expulsion stabilization. He showed no appetite for display and little interest in advancement. He repeatedly requested assignments to practical containment units rather than theological study houses.

This changed after first confirmed Auctoriel contact.

### VIII.3 First Confirmed Seraphic Contact

In 1831, during the failed expulsion of **Anrek Voss**, a thirteen-year-old vessel in the Central Region, Orveth manifested the third hand in visible form.

Anrek's possession had reached a late stage but remained theoretically recoverable. The demon had learned to exploit the attending priests' fear of killing the boy and repeatedly forced the body into positions where restraint would cause injury. Orveth entered after two failed attempts, removed his gauntlets, and restrained the boy with his own hands for nine minutes while Obsidian prepared a third Naming sequence.

Witnesses report the appearance of a large red hand emerging over Orveth's left shoulder and pinning Anrek's shadow to the floor.

This phrase was dismissed as hysteria in the first report.

It appears in five independent accounts.

The expulsion succeeded. Anrek survived with permanent voice damage and later became a lay copyist attached to the same hospital that had treated him. He sent Father Orveth a letter every year until his death in 1889. Orveth kept all fifty-eight letters.

The Ecclesiastic Surgeons began special monitoring in 1831.

The Interior Council began restricted theological inquiry in 1834.

The term **Auctoriel** first appears in Orveth's own handwriting in 1836.

## IX. ASCENSION TO RECTOR-ASCENDANT

### IX.1 Long Service Before Elevation

Halk'en Orveth was not elevated quickly.

This is sometimes explained as caution regarding his unusual manifestation. That is true, but incomplete. The other reason is simpler: he refused advancement twice.

He argued in 1849 and again in 1862 that his authority was most useful in direct field work and that administrative elevation would waste him. The first refusal was accepted. The second was recorded with irritation by Bishop Pel Varas, who wrote:

> *"Father Orveth has mistaken humility for the right to decide where the Church requires him."*

The remark reportedly amused him.

In 1877, during the **Red Choir Event** in the Northern Region, Orveth manifested five hands simultaneously for the first time in confirmed record. The event involved forty-three possessed singers in a collapsed parish choir whose shared liturgical training had been inverted into a harmonic vessel network. Ordinary Naming failed because the voices completed each other's evasions.

Orveth entered the nave alone for seven minutes.

When the doors opened, seventeen choir members were alive, six were dead, and the possessing structure had been forced into a single answerable center point. Sundersaint, then held in the reliquary vault of Saint Veyran and not yet assigned to him, was brought in afterward to complete the severance.

The Interior Council stopped treating his elevation as optional.

He was made Rector-Ascendant in 1878.

### IX.2 Receiving Sundersaint

Sundersaint was given to Father Orveth in 1881 after three years of review, two failed objections from the Keepers of Before, and one private meeting with the Primus whose record remains sealed.

The assignment was controversial.

The sword had previously been borne only by dedicated relic-Wardens under strict deployment protocols. It had never been bound to a Seraphic vessel of Dominion. The objection was obvious: a man whose manifestation could impose authority should not also carry a sword that encouraged severance.

The counterargument was also obvious: there were possession structures the Church could restrain and structures it could sever, but very few it could first make answerable and then part cleanly.

Orveth did not request the weapon.

His acceptance statement survives:

> *"If the Church gives me the Parting Mercy, then the Church must also give me men willing to take it from me if I begin to love the cut."*

The condition was recorded. It has never been tested openly.

### IX.3 Recognition as One of the Answered

The title **one of the Answered** was not formally attached to him until 1906.

This date is later than the reality. By then the Interior Council already understood that Orveth was not merely a severe authority-domain vessel. Other Seraphic contacts had been identified across the Church in fragmentary fashion. The theology of the Answered was being consolidated, quietly and with substantial disagreement.

Orveth's recognition followed the **Trial of Saint Kaldrin's Gate**, a sealed Grand Expulsion in which the sixth hand manifested tangibly for the first time before more than twenty authorized witnesses. The hostile presence, later classified as a high-order demonic fragment with distributed claim over a military hospice, had resisted all ordinary jurisdictional address by arguing through the dying soldiers' own vows of service.

The Hand of Mandate closed around the hospice bell rope.

Orveth commanded the presence to answer as trespasser rather than chaplain.

The entire building rang once.

The expulsion became possible.

Afterward, the Interior Council's language changed. Not publicly. Never publicly.

Internally, he was no longer a powerful Rector-Ascendant.

He was the vessel of Auctoriel.

## X. SELECTED CASE HISTORY

### X.1 The Black Mere Return

In 1912, Father Orveth returned to Black Mere for the first time in eighty-eight years after reports of a possession cluster near the old millrace. The village had changed little. The river had changed course. The parish registry still contained his birth entry, though the ink had faded badly enough that a younger priest initially misread his name.

The event itself was minor by Orveth's later standards. Three vessels, all recoverable. One demonic presence of moderate coherence. No casualties after his arrival.

The file matters because of what followed.

He spent two days repairing the old parish door.

When asked why, he said:

> *"It was still mine to fix."*

This sentence is frequently cited by those who argue that his identity remains properly human beneath Seraphic Weight. It should be cited carefully. The same instinct that repairs a parish door because it remains his to fix can, under greater Weight, command a broken person to stand because he has decided they remain his to correct.

### X.2 The Severance of Halvorn Bridge

In 1968, a demonic rite-state attached itself to the central suspension span of Halvorn Bridge, causing travelers who crossed after moonset to arrive without memories of the person they most loved. Civil authorities initially classified the event as Redactory sabotage.

Orveth identified the bridge itself as the claimed vessel.

The fifth hand manifested repeatedly during assessment, pointing not at the victims but at the bridge's western anchor. Sundersaint was drawn once. Orveth touched the blade to the anchor bolt and spoke no command beyond:

> *"Part from what is not yours."*

The bridge did not collapse. The rite-state did. Twenty-seven victims recovered partial memory over the following month. Four did not.

The case remains one of the clearest examples of Dominion and Severance cooperating without excessive force.

It is also why the Imperial Bridge Authority requested, unsuccessfully, that Father Orveth never again touch critical infrastructure with a greater artifact.

### X.3 The Seventh Hand at Vaul Mourne

The first confirmed manifestation of the Sovereign Palm occurred in 2013 at **Vaul Mourne**, a fortified hospice-priory on the northern approach to the Threshold.

The event began as a standard evacuation during Dark-Side pressure escalation and became a multi-vessel possession when the lower wards failed to seal. A fragment-class presence entered through the dying and attempted to claim the hospice under the category of mercy, arguing through the mouths of patients that removal from suffering constituted rightful care.

Three Votaries died before Orveth arrived.

The seventh hand appeared during the third hour.

Reliable witness description from Ecclesiastic Surgeon Tam Vey:

> *"It did not arrive like force. It arrived like the end of argument."*

Under the Sovereign Palm, the hostile presence was made answerable as a trespasser upon mercy rather than an expression of it. Sundersaint severed the claim through the hospice's altar cloth, which had become the rite-state's symbolic center.

Forty-six patients died.

One hundred and twelve survived.

Orveth did not speak for nine days afterward except in confession.

### X.4 The Orison Dispute

In 2059, Father Orveth publicly contradicted Rector-Ascendant Delian Orison during an Interior Council review concerning the forced investiture of a protection-domain candidate who had repeatedly refused permanent Obsidian formation.

Orison argued that the candidate's capacity created obligation.

Orveth replied:

> *"A burden placed without rightful permission is not office. It is seizure."*

The statement has become central to modern formation ethics and is often used by Orveth's defenders to prove his distinction from more coercive severe-domain vessels.

The same review records that Orveth recommended the candidate be released to ordinary parish oversight under monitoring.

The candidate survived, never joined Obsidian, and later became a schoolmaster.

Father Orveth visited him once, forty years later, and blessed his retirement.

### X.5 The Silent Procession

In 2166, during a major ecclesiastical procession in Kalt'sen, all visible members of Obsidian within the route fell silent at the same moment. Civilians assumed this was ceremonial. It was not.

A demonic mimic had entered the procession by wearing the grief of a woman whose son had died in Threshold service. The mimic carried no body of its own and moved through sympathetic attention. Speaking its presence aloud would have spread it.

The Veiled Hand manifested visibly to at least thirty civilians, though most later described it as a trick of red glass in the Moonlight. Orveth walked the length of the procession without drawing Sundersaint. At the central square he placed his bare hand on the grieving woman's shoulder and said:

> *"This sorrow is hers. You will not use it."*

The Grasping Hand closed around empty air behind her.

The mimic dispersed without public panic.

The woman never learned the full nature of what occurred. Orveth arranged for her son's name to be restored to the Threshold memorial after discovering it had been misspelled.

### X.6 Consultation Regarding Father Viktor

The Interior Council requested Father Orveth's private opinion on Vaey Viktor in 2198.

The meeting lasted sixteen minutes.

No transcript exists. Mother Calienne Voss's later marginal note records only Orveth's conclusion:

> *"Measurement has placed itself where judgment should stand. Do not ask Dominion to bless cowardice by calling it patience."*

No action followed.

This is one of several reasons Father Orveth's current loyalty to the Church should not be mistaken for institutional comfort.

## XI. PSYCHOLOGICAL AND PASTORAL PROFILE

Father Orveth has endured four centuries of Seraphic Weight without entering the known failure states associated with severe-domain Crowned vessels. This is remarkable. It is not miraculous enough to remove concern.

His stabilizing traits are consistent:

- strong sacramental discipline;
- low vanity;
- stable confessor relationships;
- refusal to treat power as self-expression;
- unusually high tolerance for being corrected by subordinates in narrow technical matters;
- persistent memory of ordinary parish life;
- aversion to unnecessary touch;
- explicit fear of loving command for its own sake.

His dangerous traits are equally consistent:

- deep contempt for authority refused out of comfort;
- tendency to interpret crisis through burden and office;
- limited patience for institutional hesitation;
- capacity to impose unbearable responsibility on those he believes can bear it;
- difficulty distinguishing rest from abandonment when the subject possesses unusual capacity;
- willingness to stand in judgment when other authorities fail to act.

Unlike Father Viktor, Orveth does not appear to confuse one Seraphic domain for the whole of justice. He knows Dominion is not mercy, not potential, not truth, not healing, not light, and not the Sovereign entire. He states this often.

The concern is not doctrinal ignorance.

The concern is that, in the field, Dominion solves problems.

The more often a domain solves problems no other authority can solve, the more tempting it becomes to let the domain define the problem.

This is the drift being monitored.

## XII. RELATIONSHIP WITH THE ARCH-PATRIARCH

The public doctrine surrounding the Primus states that the office stands in unique relation to the Sovereign. It does not define this relation with technical precision. Restricted discourse is less certain than public liturgy.

Father Orveth has never publicly questioned the status of the Primus.

Privately, he is careful.

Three holders of the office of Primus have held office during Orveth's service. He has served all three. He obeyed **Primus Soren IV** with visible ease, argued frequently with **Primus Melchis Vey**, and appears to regard the current Primus, **Caldrin II**, with a form of wary respect that neither side has attempted to warm into affection.

In 2187, during a closed council concerning the Answered, Caldrin II reportedly asked Orveth whether Auctoriel recognized the papal office.

Orveth answered:

> *"Dominion recognizes office when office bears its burden."*

The council record notes a silence of twelve seconds.

No one present requested clarification.

## XIII. WHAT HE BELIEVES

Father Orveth's theology can be summarized in a sentence:

> Authority is not the right to command. It is the obligation to become answerable for what command does.

This belief has saved lives.

It has also made him relentless.

He does not admire power. He admires borne office. He has little patience for nobles who inherit authority as decoration, priests who use rank as shelter, Votaries who enjoy fear, or civilians who demand rescue while refusing all obligation to those beside them. He is capable of enormous tenderness toward the weak. He is far less gentle with the capable who pretend to be weak because weakness would excuse them.

He believes that some burdens are not chosen because choice is not the root of all rightful obligation. A parent does not choose every duty of parenthood. A priest does not choose every soul who enters the confessional. A commander does not choose every life placed under command. A divine vessel does not choose that Heaven touched them.

But he also believes that burden placed without rightful permission becomes violence.

This distinction is the line on which most of his moral life balances.

The line has held for centuries.

Lines can wear thin.

## XIV. WHAT HE DOES NOT KNOW, OR WILL NOT ACCEPT

He does not know, or will not accept:

- that some people survive only by laying down burdens they were never able to carry;
- that peace can be a vocation rather than a refusal;
- that not every abandoned office should be restored;
- that some commands become less holy by being obeyed under terror;
- that his gentleness does not always prevent coercion;
- that a person made capable for ten seconds may spend decades grieving the return to ordinary limitation;
- that the Church's willingness to deploy him is not proof that deployment is always right;
- that his fear of loving command may itself become a way of continuing to command while feeling absolved of pride;
- that the seventh hand may not need to corrupt him in order to use him too completely.

He knows Auctoriel is not the Sovereign.

This knowledge may save him.

It may not be enough.

## XV. CURRENT ASSESSMENT

Rector-Ascendant Halk'en Orveth remains one of the most powerful living assets of the Church and one of the few individuals capable of resolving authority-resistant possession structures without catastrophic civilian loss. His control remains acceptable by current Ecclesiastic Surgical standards. His confessor reports continued humility, though the term should be handled carefully when applied to a man whose humility includes allowing himself to be used as a Seraphic instrument of command.

The Sevenfold Hand has not destabilized beyond documented limits. The seventh expression remains rare. Sundersaint remains cooperative under his bearing and has not shown signs of Severance-pressure overriding his judgment. The relationship between Dominion and Severance continues to require review after every major deployment.

He is not to be deployed for political intimidation, public demonstration, relic theater, noble dispute settlement, or ordinary heresy inquiry.

He is not to be asked to bless institutional cowardice by inaction.

He is not to be left alone with a severe-domain Crowned under active moral dispute unless at least one Ecclesiastic Surgeon and one confessor priest are present.

He is not to be allowed to decide, without review, that the Church has failed to bear its own office.

That final prohibition is unwritten in every version of this file except this one.

It is the most important.

Father Orveth is faithful. He is disciplined. He is necessary.

He is also what happens when the hand of Heaven remains in the world long enough to learn the weight of human institutions and still closes anyway.

He should be honored.

He should be watched.

He should never be mistaken for safe.
