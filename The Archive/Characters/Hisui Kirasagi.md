## PREAMBLE — HOW SHE CAME TO BE HERE

*The file that led to Hisui Kirasagi was not one I opened. It was opened for me.*

*CLOCKWORK's foreign-anomaly screening operates on a standing protocol: immigration applications passing through the empire's border administration are flagged when physiological markers in the applicant's medical intake documentation fall outside any standard biological profile. The flags arrive quarterly. Most are unremarkable — metabolic anomalies, inherited resonance sensitivity in lineages that never produced practitioners, the occasional misclassified minor Dive complication that border physicians recorded without understanding. I review them myself. It has always seemed imprudent to delegate this category of assessment to someone whose threshold of interest might differ from mine.*

*The flag for Hi'sui Kirasagi arrived in the second quarter of 2197. The physiological profile the border physicians had recorded — because they are trained to be thorough, and she was thorough enough in her own presentation that nothing in her manner prompted them to be less so — contained structural markers they classified as a minor circulatory irregularity. It was not a circulatory irregularity. What they had measured, without knowing what they were measuring, was the vascular integration of a resonance-active organism that had been symbiotic with its host's blood for longer than she had been alive.*

*I recognized it because I had seen something like it once before, in documentation from an ancient-site excavation the Company conducted in Hikari's compressed central corridor in year 2162. The biological material recovered at that site had degraded past the threshold of functional analysis, but the structural signature had been catalogued. What the border physician's intake form described was that signature in a living host.*

*I pulled her full immigration file the same evening. Hikari national, harbor-city origin, Tsukimiya. Kirasagi family — a name I did not recognize, which told me something: the Company maintains comprehensive files on Hikari's aristocratic service lineages, and if the Kirasagi were not in them, they had kept themselves obscure by discipline rather than insignificance. I found them in the restricted annals of the House of Shinzui's private records, which the Company acquired through commercial-intelligence channels during the 2170 Shinzui succession dispute. Five hundred years of retainer service. An internal family record that used the phrase "the pact of the Bloom" with the calm familiarity of a tradition so old its origins had ceased to require explanation.*

*I spent three months building a working theory of what the organism was before I made any placement decision. I used CLOCKWORK's assessment instruments at a remove the subject was not aware of. I obtained the Kirasagi household's restricted medical records through three separate commercial-intelligence intermediaries. I consulted the 2162 excavation documentation. I do not make decisions about people based on incomplete pictures if a more complete picture is available and time permits.*

*What I concluded: the organism — the Abyssal Flower, in the Kirasagi's own naming — was older than the family that carried it. Older, in my assessment, than Hikari as a civilization. It was resonance-active in a way that no contemporary framework could account for, and it carried structural properties — specifically, its conductivity relationship to resonance-gradient fluctuation — that made it a passive sensor of unusual precision and, under Calling conditions, a potential amplification hazard I was not comfortable leaving unsupervised in the broader world.*

*The placement question arrived independently of all of this. I was already building the architecture around Ec'lesia's household — Sai'ken had been positioned in 2197, and the monitoring infrastructure was in place. What the household lacked was a presence that could function as a passive topology sensor without requiring active briefing. The Apocachynthion's instruments do not register the Flower. Whatever Hisui carried into a room, she carried invisibly to every institutional instrument that might otherwise flag the anomaly. The Flower's internal state, however, responds with measurable precision to the resonance topology around it. She was, functionally, a passive sensor I could position without anyone — including her — knowing she was one.*

*The chain I constructed is genuine at every step. This is important to me for reasons that are not solely operational. The placement agency that recommended her is a real agency with a real reputation. Hisui Kirasagi is genuinely qualified for high-house domestic service — her training is authentic, her record is clean, her references are from actual households in Hikari. I did not manufacture a person. I identified a person already entering the Empire through legitimate channels and arranged that she would be recommended for this specific posting rather than the three others she was equally qualified for.*

*The mechanism: the placement agency maintains a working relationship with a commercial-contact office the Company operates under a subsidiary name in Tsukimiya's harbor district. The office does not conduct recruitment. It does not interact with applicants. What it does, through a series of ordinary commercial introductions, is ensure that the agencies it works with develop positive impressions of candidates the Company finds interesting. The household administrator who hired Hisui believes he found an excellent candidate through normal channels. He is correct. I made normal channels deliver her here.*

*The Flower's behavior in proximity to Ec'lesia's topology — the structural dampening effect I suspected but could not confirm without direct observation — has been confirmed across two years of passive monitoring. She is calmer here than any Kirasagi household record shows her to have been anywhere else in her life. This is, I note, a good outcome for a woman who did not ask for what she carries and has managed it alone since childhood.*

*She does not know I placed her. Sai'ken does not know I placed her. Ec'lesia certainly does not know. This is the architecture as I built it.*

*— E.W., year 2200*

## II. THE NATION OF HIKARI — BRIEF CONTEXTUAL NOTE

*The minimum necessary to understand where this woman comes from and what that origin means for what she carries.*

Hikari occupies the Twilight Band segment west of Aurora, distinguished by three features relevant to this file.

Its geography is compressed. The central corridor narrows significantly in Hikari's segment — the bright-side and dark-side gradients press closer together than they do elsewhere, producing a habitable zone that is fertile but shallow. The population has concentrated along the Darkmargin coast and in steep river valleys, producing a civilization that is dense, maritime, and acutely aware of its borders.

Its institutions handle Redactory differently. There is no Apocachynthion equivalent. Redactory governance runs through independent schools and lineage traditions rather than a centralized regulatory body. The One Church operates a small mission presence alongside Hikari's indigenous religious practice — a complex tradition centered on the concept of *continuity through indebtedness* — and neither has resolved the theological disagreement between them.

Its aristocratic houses maintain retainer families bound through generations of contractual obligation — as household staff, advisors, bodyguards, and, in the older lineages, as bearers of inherited responsibilities whose original terms have been lost to time but whose obligations persist. The Kirasagi are one such family. They have served a particular Hikari house for at least five centuries, and the nature of that service became something considerably more than conventional retainership long before anyone alive today could remember when the change occurred.

## III. LINEAGE AND THE ABYSSAL FLOWER

### III.1 The Kirasagi Inheritance

The Kirasagi family's retainer bond is to the House of Shinzui — one of Hikari's seven hereditary high houses, associated with maritime commerce and the administration of Tsukimiya's harbor district. A Redactor lineage of moderate power; significant enough to command hereditary service, not significant enough to warrant attention beyond Hikari's borders.

The Kirasagi have served them for five hundred years. At some point in the early centuries of the arrangement, that service shifted from conventional domestic retainership to what the Kirasagi call *the pact of the Bloom*. The Bloom is the Abyssal Flower. The exact terms of the original pact are not known to anyone currently living, including the Kirasagi.

### III.2 What the Abyssal Flower Is

The Abyssal Flower is a resonance-active organism. Not a divine fragment. Not a Redactory construct. It is biological — or was, at some point, biological in a way that has since been complicated by centuries of sustained resonance integration.

It exists as a symbiotic presence within the bloodline. Each generation inherits it through *seeding* — exposure of the newborn to the previous host's resonance signature at a specific developmental window. If the child's recursive architecture is compatible, the organism takes root. If not, the child dies. The mortality rate across documented Kirasagi generations is approximately thirty percent. The family has maintained the practice for five hundred years.

### III.3 What the Flower Does

The organism manifests through the host's resonance signature as projections of crimson-black fractal structures the Kirasagi call *petals*.

**Petal Projection.** Externalized coherence pressure discharged as sharp-edged constructs. These cut — severing molecular coherence at the point of contact. Targets struck at close range also experience short-term memory disruption: a localized impression fracture that degrades the most recent mnemonic configurations. This secondary effect operates at the recursive level, not the physical one.

**Hemoturgy.** The organism's resonance interacts with iron-carrying protein structures in blood. The host can accelerate or suppress local circulation through directed coherence pressure — promoting coagulation, increasing bleeding, or destabilizing the resonance equilibrium of blood within a living body to produce systemic collapse. The last application is classified in Kirasagi household records as a last-measure capacity.

**Bloom Field.** Petal-structures projected in sufficient density to create a zone of trajectory and perception distortion. Incoming objects encounter competing resonance gradients that deflect their paths. Observers attempting to perceive through the field find their own recursive processing of sensory data disrupted.

**Renewal Weave.** Petals deployed as a biological repair scaffold — a resonance-active lattice that bridges a wound and provides a coherence framework around which the body's natural repair processes can organize. The weave does not heal; it provides structure the body fills. If the body has not completed its own repair before the weave degrades, the wound reopens.

### III.4 What the Flower Costs

**Overbloom.** High-output use draws from the host's own recursive coherence. Sustained activation depletes that coherence faster than it regenerates. The host experiences this as exhaustion deepening toward structural fragility — not merely physical weakness but a reduction in the density of their own recursive architecture. Repeated Overbloom events produce cumulative coherence deficit.

**Murmuring.** The organism retains resonance impressions of previous hosts — structural echoes from prior generations that persist within its own coherence architecture. The host perceives these as whispered voices, fragmentary images, and emotional textures without originating context. Constant and low-level under normal conditions. Under stress, Overbloom, or coherence deficit, the Murmuring intensifies. In extreme cases the host's recursive identity becomes difficult to distinguish from the accumulated echo-impressions of the dead.

**Consumption Risk.** If the host's recursive coherence falls below the threshold required to contain the organism's growth, the Flower expands unchecked. The host's body becomes substrate. The result is structural dissolution — the person is consumed as the organism colonizes their architecture. What remains is petals. The Kirasagi household records document seven such instances across five hundred years.

### III.5 Origin — Working Theory

The Abyssal Flower is not, in my assessment, indigenous to Hikari. Its resonance signature carries structural characteristics inconsistent with any native biological profile catalogued within the Twilight Band. It is old — older than the Kirasagi, and possibly older than Hikari as a civilization.

My working theory is that it originated from one of the ancient civilization's biological-research sites. The Company has catalogued ancient-site ruins across the Twilight Band for centuries. A subset show signs of biological experimentation, concentrated in regions of extreme gradient proximity. Hikari's compressed corridor places habitable terrain closer to both gradients than any other segment of the Band. The structural match is plausible. I hold it as theory and treat it accordingly.

The Kirasagi believe the Flower is ancestral — a gift from a figure their tradition names *the Gardener*, a semi-mythological benefactor said to have visited the family in its earliest years and planted something in their blood that would bloom forever. The Gardener, in my assessment, was almost certainly a contact event with a biological-resonance artifact of the ancient civilization, experienced through the cultural lens of a family that had no framework for what it actually was and built one that served.

## IV. BIOGRAPHICAL RECORD

### IV.1 Early Life

Hisui Kirasagi was born in 2176 in Tsukimiya, second child of the Kirasagi branch then serving in the Shinzui household's harbor-district residence. She was seeded with the Flower in infancy. She survived.

She was told about the Flower before she could fully understand what she was being told. The Kirasagi do not conceal the inheritance from their children — the Murmuring begins at birth, and a child who hears voices no one else hears will ask eventually. She was told young, in the manner the Kirasagi give their children information: as fact, not as warning. Warnings are for people who have a choice.

She trained in the Kirasagi household tradition from early childhood — domestic service and household management alongside close-quarters and concealed-blade work, embedded in the curriculum without being separated from it. The Kirasagi do not distinguish between service and protection. The person you serve is the person you guard.

### IV.2 The 2191 Event

In year 2191, a Calling event of unusual intensity struck Hikari's compressed corridor. Hisui was fifteen.

What the Kirasagi household records describe as a severe Bloom instability event was, in my assessment, a structural amplification crisis: the Flower's resonance-conductive properties interacting with the Calling's gradient fluctuation and producing output the host's body could not contain. The petals erupted from her forearms involuntarily, lacerating the surface tissue in their emergence. She did not lose consciousness. She was in controlled confinement within the Kirasagi compound for eleven days afterward. The scars are still visible.

The Kirasagi's explanation — Flower displeasure at confinement — is culturally coherent and mechanically wrong. The mechanism is structural, not emotional.

### IV.3 Departure and Arrival

In year 2197, the Kirasagi family designated Hisui as their *far bloom* — the family member sent abroad in the tradition that distributes the lineage's inherited burden across greater geographic range. Her brother was the primary host candidate; he could not travel due to a sustained Overbloom condition that made extended removal from the compound medically imprudent.

She was not consulted in any meaningful sense. She was designated. The *far bloom* is not a punishment. It is a structural function of the lineage. She was the one who could go. So she went.

She entered the empire through Kalt'sen's northern immigration processing center in the autumn of 2197, registered with the placement agency the following month, and was recommended to the Wonderwall cadet-branch household administrator in early 2198 through channels that were, at every visible step, entirely legitimate.

## V. PHYSICAL RECORD

Height: tall for a Hikari woman. Build: lean and precise — the kind of economy of movement that is trained rather than natural, visible in the way she occupies space even when still.

**Skin:** Pale with faint warmth. Under strain — physical exertion, emotional duress, or Flower activation — a subtle marbling of fine crimson veins becomes visible along the forearms and backs of the hands: vascular integration expressing through the surface tissue. Not visible under normal conditions.

**Hair:** Black. Cut in the short, precise style the Hikari service tradition calls *the working line* — hair that does not interfere with labor, require maintenance during a working day, or present a grip point in close-quarters situations. She maintains it herself, cutting it every three weeks with the same shears she uses for the household mending.

**Eyes:** Deep, near-black under passive conditions — the trained stillness of someone who learned that household staff does not show its thoughts on its face. Under Flower activation or acute stress: violet-crimson, with fractal flares at the iris margin that bloom outward. She has not activated the Flower's external manifestation in Kalt'sen. The eye-change has occurred twice in the observation period — both times during sleep, both times transient. She does not know I observed it.

**Scars:** Both forearms carry the healed lacerations from the 2191 event — thin, pale, numerous, arranged in a pattern that follows the Flower's petal-eruption trajectories. She wears long sleeves consistently. In Hikari's service tradition, this is standard dress. In the Empire, it reads as personal preference. She has not been asked about the scars.

**Notable Object:** A lacquered black hairpin of Hikari manufacture — a narrow spike of compressed lacquerwork over a steel core, decorated with a single inlaid crimson bead. A resonance trigger: a material object whose structural properties reduce the coherence cost of initiating the Flower's combat-ready externalization sequence. Drawing it from her hair initiates that sequence.

She has not drawn it in Kalt'sen.

## VI. PERSONALITY

**Surface presentation:** Disciplined. Professional. Reserved in the manner the Kirasagi tradition produces — not cold, but contained. Her Imperial Common is fluent and slightly more precise than a native speaker's, as though she arrived at each sentence through a shorter but more deliberate path. She does not volunteer information. She answers questions with the minimum necessary. She does not lie. She omits. The Kirasagi tradition teaches that what is not asked is not owed.

**Private behavior:** The observation instruments I have placed in the household are passive and non-intrusive — resonance-detection only. What the topology tells me: she is warmer than she presents.

With Ec'lesia, the Flower settles into its calmest sustained state when Hisui is in active attendance. Whether this is the Remainder's dampening effect or genuine emotional investment producing resonance calm, I cannot separate. I suspect both. The care, however, is real and does not require the topology to confirm it.

With Sai'ken, the Flower does not calm. It oscillates — not dangerously, but in a rhythmic pattern that suggests the organism is responding to something in his structural profile with what I can only describe as attention. His traversal-phase displacement produces a localized topology distortion. The Flower finds it interesting.

The two of them are comfortable with each other in a way that exceeds professional routine. Both carry inherited anomalies they did not choose. Both serve a household they entered through channels more complex than they appear. She calls him *the scarecrow* — a reference to his lean build and his habit of standing very still in doorways. He responds with the particular quality of amusement his dossier identifies as the closest thing to unguarded behavior he produces. I note this. I do not interfere with it.

## VII. WHAT SHE KNOWS AND WHAT SHE DOES NOT

**What she knows:**

She knows the Abyssal Flower is inside her. She knows it can kill her — that three in ten children who receive it die before their first year, and that sustained overuse can trigger a consumption event that leaves nothing behind but petals. She was told these things as facts.

She knows the Apocachynthion would classify her as an anomaly. She does not know the specific institutional framework, but she understands — correctly — that carrying a resonance organism produced by neither the Dive nor any sanctioned institution would not be received well by any authority in the Empire. She keeps the Flower concealed. In her frame of reference, this is not deception. It is the discipline of a service family: what the house does not show is what the house protects.

She knows she was sent abroad because her brother could not be. She does not resent this.

**What she does not know:**

She does not know that Em'manuel Kael Wonderwall exists as anything other than the name of the family's distant patriarch — a figure she has never met and has no reason to expect she will. She does not know that the placement chain that brought her here was shaped by someone who had studied her medical-intake documentation months before the placement agency received her file.

She does not know what the Remainder is. She does not know what Ec'lesia carries. She does not know what Sai'ken is. She does not know that the calm she feels in this household — the particular quiet of the Flower's internal state that she has never experienced anywhere else in her life — is produced by something in Ec'lesia's topology that no one in the house has been told about.

She does not know the Flower's true origin, nor that its resonance is structurally similar enough to BrightCrystal's conductivity properties that a sustained Calling event could trigger an amplification crisis. She experienced this once, at fifteen. The Kirasagi's explanation was Flower displeasure. It was not.

## VIII. ASSESSMENT — WHY SHE IS THERE

I placed Hisui Kirasagi in Ec'lesia's household for three reasons, which I record in descending order of how comfortable I am stating them.

**First:** diagnostic. The Flower's resonance conductivity makes Hisui a passive sensor for the topology around Ec'lesia. What I learn from the Flower's behavior in proximity to the Remainder is data I cannot obtain from any other source, including Sai'ken, whose own anomalous profile produces a different category of interaction.

**Second:** containment. If proximity to the Remainder's density genuinely dampens the Flower's volatility — and two years of monitoring suggest it does — then Hisui is safer here than anywhere else she could be. I have placed someone in a position where their condition is measurably improved by their assignment. This is not altruism. It is efficient architecture. But it is also a good outcome for a woman who did not ask for what she carries.

**Third:** the one I am least comfortable stating. Ec'lesia lives in a household where her mother manages without understanding, her sister is too young to be involved, and Sai'ken is assigned protective function. No one occupies the role of someone who is simply present, without agenda, without mandate, without the weight of what they were placed there to observe. What I have observed over two years is that Hisui's service has become something else — something that functions, operationally, as care. She attends to Ec'lesia with an attention that exceeds professional obligation. She has noticed things about Ec'lesia's routines, her stress patterns, her quiet moments of difficulty, that the household administrator's notes do not record.

Whether this is the Flower responding to the Remainder's field, or whether it is simply what happens when a kind person serves someone who needs kindness, I cannot determine. I suspect both. I do not consider the distinction urgent.

## IX. RISK ASSESSMENT

**Calling vulnerability:** High. Any Calling event of moderate or greater intensity will interact with the Flower's resonance-conductive properties. Proximity to Ec'lesia's topology may dampen this, but I cannot confirm it under actual Calling conditions — no significant Calling has occurred since Hisui's placement.

**Institutional detection:** Low under current conditions. The Apocachynthion's screening instruments do not register the Flower. The Church's Heralds detect fragment-class presences; the Flower is not a fragment. Stasis would detect the anomaly under close-range scan, which they have no reason to conduct. The greatest detection risk is a visible activation event — petals, eye change, or Overbloom — in the presence of a witness who knows what they are looking at.

**Consumption risk:** Low. The Flower's current behavioral profile — calm, stable, reduced Murmuring — is, per Kirasagi household records, the lowest-risk state the organism exhibits. If proximity to Ec'lesia is genuinely stabilizing the Flower, Hisui is currently in the safest condition she has ever experienced. This could change if she is separated from the household for an extended period.

**Interpersonal risk:** Moderate. The risk is not that Hisui will harm someone. The risk is that she will form attachments to people in a household she was placed in without being told why, and that when the truth of the arrangement is eventually revealed — as it must be — the discovery will damage something she has built in good faith.

I am aware of this. I am aware that the same risk applies to Sai'ken, to Ec'lesia, and to every arrangement I have constructed around people who deserve better than to discover that the architecture of their lives was shaped by a man twelve centuries old who had his reasons.

I have had this conversation with myself before. I have not resolved it. I do not expect to.
