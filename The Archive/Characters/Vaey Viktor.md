## PREAMBLE - WHAT THIS FILE IS FOR

*This is not the Obsidian Rite service record of Father Vaey Viktor. That document exists and remains formally unblemished, which is itself part of the problem this file exists to preserve.*

*It is not the canonization record. No formal canonization has occurred. No formal censure has occurred either. The absence of both outcomes should not be mistaken for neutrality.*

*This file exists because Father Viktor has become too significant, too dangerous, and too institutionally protected to remain distributed across incident reports, retired-assessor notes, confessor summaries, and sealed homicide correspondences that do not name themselves as such. He requires a dossier that treats him as a living person, a former priest of long service, a confirmed Beatified vessel, and a serial killer operating beneath the tolerance of the Church's own reluctance.*

*The methodological problem is obvious. The Church made use of him for forty years. The Rite trained him, trusted him, consulted him, and sent him into rooms where frightened families were told that his judgment could decide whether a child was blessed, endangered, possessed, or worth removing from ordinary life. He was useful before he became holy. He was holy before he became intolerable. No one in this archive should pretend those were unrelated stages.*

## II. WHAT HE IS

Vaey Viktor is a retired priest of the Obsidian Rite, a former assessor of saint-candidates, and a confirmed vessel of Meruel, the angelic Measurement of Potential. He spent the greater part of his adult life looking at people after miracles had touched them and deciding what the Church should do with what remained.

This fact cannot be separated from what he has become.

He did not enter the Rite as a front-line Votary. He was not known first for strength, radiance, exorcistic authority, or heroic conduct along the Threshold. His gift was colder and rarer: discernment. He could enter a room where a child had spoken in a voice not their own, where a dying woman had healed by accident, where a soldier had survived something no body should survive, where a minor saint-cult had begun forming around a frightened young man in a rural parish, and he could determine what sort of pressure had entered the vessel.

Angelic. Demonic. Sight-adjacent. False. Unstable. Politically convenient but spiritually empty. Genuinely holy but psychologically unready. Recoverable. Dangerous. Worth taking. Worth watching. Worth burying.

For forty years, the Church asked him these questions.

For forty years, he answered.

The repeated act shaped him. Each assessment required him to reconstruct the distance between present condition and possible sanctity. Each failed candidate taught him to see what a person might have become if they had been taken earlier, trained better, broken less, obeyed more completely, or refused less stubbornly. Each successful saint confirmed the opposite lesson: that potential, when disciplined, becomes proof.

By the end of his service, Father Viktor was no longer merely assessing potential. His recursive singularity had become organized around the act of measurement itself. Meruel did not fall upon an unprepared man. Meruel found a mind that had spent four decades becoming a suitable instrument.

What followed was not possession. It was not demonic erosion. It was not one of the Obsidian Rite's rare Answered-class or Lucent-class Seraphic extremes. It was not even, in the Church's most comfortable language, corruption. It was Beatified stabilization along a real angelic fragment-integration the Church did not know how to pastor once it became severe.

Father Viktor did not stop believing.

He became too precise in the belief.

## III. APPEARANCE, TEMPERAMENT, AND SOCIAL PRESENCE

He appears to be in his mid-sixties, and this appearance is accurate enough for all ordinary purposes. He is not preserved by his fusion into false youth. Meruel has not made him beautiful. It has made him exact.

He is tall, though age has introduced a slight forward inclination to his posture that reads less as weakness than as the habit of a man accustomed to leaning over documents, bedsides, confession screens, and examination tables. His build is thin to the point of severity. His shoulders are narrow. His hands are long, veined, and dry, with the careful stillness of someone who learned early that touch, in the wrong moment, can be mistaken for either comfort or claim.

His hair is crimson with strands of white, formerly dark, worn neatly and without vanity. His face is lined most deeply around the mouth and eyes. The lines do not soften him. They make his expressions more legible, which is not always the same thing as making them kinder. His eyes remain very clear. Witnesses frequently describe them before they describe anything he says.

In ordinary dress he favors black clerical clothing, old but meticulously maintained. His collar is always correct. His sleeves are always clean. He wears no ostentatious relics and no public saint's adornment. The only consistent object on his person is a narrow silver measuring chain, worn wound twice around the left wrist beneath the sleeve. The chain is not an official instrument of the Rite. It has become, through use, a devotional habit and a killing implement.

His physical manner is quiet. He does not intrude upon rooms. He arranges himself within them. He speaks softly, never quickly, and with the slightly old-fashioned diction of a priest trained before the current generation of Obsidian formation reforms. Younger clergy often find him reassuring before they learn enough to fear him.

His courtesy is genuine. This is one of the facts that complicates every record written about him by someone who has met him more than once. He thanks attendants. He remembers the names of junior priests. He does not humiliate the frightened. He is gentle with children under early manifestation pressure. He can sit for hours with a family in crisis and leave them calmer than they were when he arrived.

The same man has killed people after telling them exactly what they could have been.

When Meruel expresses through him, he becomes physically steadier. The slight age-tremor in his hands vanishes. His spine straightens. His eyes pale toward a cold gold-white, not luminous enough to be theatrical but bright enough to look wrong in low light. A thin geometry sometimes appears behind him: not wings, not a halo in the devotional sense, but an arrangement of arcs, ratios, suspended lines, and incomplete circles. Several witnesses have independently described the impression of a measuring instrument pretending to be holy.

His anger is almost undetectable. It presents as disappointment.

This is more dangerous.

## IV. THE SERAPHIC MEASURE AND MERUEL

### IV.1 The Constellation of Just Measure

The angelic fragment Meruel belongs to a Seraphic Constellation whose central Seraphim has not yet been assigned a stable formal name in Church doctrine. Restricted marginalia most often calls it **the Nameless Seraphim of Just Measure**, though this phrase is descriptive rather than liturgical.

That Seraphim is the constellation's **Cynosure** or **central star**. It is not justice in the civic sense. It is not law, not punishment, and not mercy by itself. It is the divine principle by which a being is measured against what is true of it: what it has done, what it owes, what it could bear, what it has refused, what remains recoverable, and what has become false beyond restoration.

The constellation is understood to have six major angelic fragments, functioning as **Cardinal Stars** beneath that Cynosure. The names below are the current restricted working names used in this dossier. Only Meruel is confirmed in relation to Father Viktor.

| Cardinal Star | Measurement | Working Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Meruel** | Potential / Completion | Measures the distance between what a person is and what they could coherently become. |
| **Sathiel** | Guilt / Moral Weight | Measures what a person has knowingly done and the burden that remains attached to the act. |
| **Oriel** | Readiness / Worthiness | Measures whether a person is prepared to receive power, office, knowledge, sainthood, or burden. |
| **Thamiel** | Harm / Consequence | Measures damage caused by action, negligence, cowardice, or permitted decay. |
| **Cazriel** | Integrity / Truth of Self | Measures whether a person is internally aligned or living as a contradiction. |
| **Nehariel** | Mercy / Recoverability | Measures whether a person can still be restored without falsehood. |

The danger of Father Viktor is that he is aligned to one measurement and behaves as though it were the whole of justice.

Meruel shows him potential. It does not show him mercy. It does not show him the full moral weight of a life. It does not tell him whether a person's refusal was cowardice, trauma, prudence, exhaustion, love, or an act of self-preservation that no angel of Potential is suited to evaluate alone.

Father Viktor knows this doctrinally.

He does not accept it operationally.

### IV.2 Meruel's Expression Through Viktor

Meruel expresses through Father Viktor as assessment, completion pressure, and the perception of unrealized form.

He does not see the future. He does not see destiny. What he sees is worse for the people he judges: the coherent shape of what they could have become under conditions of discipline, opportunity, courage, training, and sustained alignment with their strongest internal architecture.

In his own reports, written before the killings began, he called this phenomenon **the completed silhouette**.

The term is apt. He perceives a person alongside the outline of their possible self. The greater the distance between the living person and the completed silhouette, the more severe the internal pressure becomes. In ordinary assessment work, this made him exceptionally good at identifying saint-candidates whose present fear disguised immense spiritual capacity. In retirement, it became the basis of a private theology of disposal.

He measures. He compares. He decides whether the distance is tolerable.

When he decides it is not, someone dies.

## V. EARLY LIFE AND ORDINATION

The surviving birth record places Vaey Viktor in a minor river district north-east of Kalt'sen, in a community too small to preserve much more than parish ledgers, flood records, and the names of priests who passed through. His parents, Revan Viktor and Maela Viktor, appear in those ledgers as ordinary people: literate, devout, economically stable enough to send a child into Church education, not prominent enough to draw House attention.

The absence of an apostrophe in his recorded name has produced occasional administrative comment. The original parish documents preserve it as **Vaey Viktor**, with no Synus-style alteration. Later ecclesiastical copies retain the form. No correction was ever made. The best explanation is clerical inheritance from a non-imperial family line absorbed into the Central Region two generations prior. Father Viktor himself has never treated the matter as interesting.

He entered minor seminary at fourteen. The earliest surviving formation notes do not describe him as warm, inspired, or visionary. They describe him as attentive, severe with himself, and unusually capable of remembering not only what a person said in confession-adjacent instruction, but what they seemed to have avoided saying.

At nineteen he was transferred to a Church analytical house in Kalt'sen after an incident involving another novice, Dalen Orr, who had begun producing small involuntary miracles during fever. Three supervising priests recorded inconsistent accounts of the event. Viktor's account was the only one that separated the miracle from the boy's fear, the fear from the surrounding liturgical expectation, and the expectation from the pressure that had begun to organize around him.

The report was not kind.

It was correct.

Dalen Orr was removed from ordinary formation and later confirmed as a minor protection-domain vessel. He survived. The priests who had initially mishandled the case did not forgive Viktor quickly.

The Obsidian Rite noticed.

## VI. THE OBSIDIAN RITE YEARS

### VI.1 Entry Into Assessment Work

Vaey Viktor was ordained at twenty-two and attached to the Obsidian Rite within the year, not as a candidate for field Votary formation but as an assessment specialist. His first superior was Rector-Ascendant Halvek Sorn, a protection-domain veteran of the northern formations whose surviving notes show early irritation with Viktor's lack of battlefield instinct and later reliance on precisely the same quality.

Halvek's third-year evaluation contains the sentence most often quoted in internal summaries:

> *"Viktor does not console the room. He makes the room legible. This is less pleasant and more useful."*

His early work placed him in the aftermath of minor miracles, uncontrolled Crownings, suspected demonic mimicries, and parish-level saint rumors. He traveled constantly. His task was not to inspire devotion but to decide what the devotion had attached itself to.

Several names recur in his service period and should be preserved here:

- **Rector-Ascendant Halvek Sorn**, first Obsidian superior; protection-domain vessel; later died in Threshold service.
- **Mother Calienne Voss**, confessor-priest and later Diaconate critic; one of the first to warn that Viktor's assessment language had become punitive.
- **Sister Aramina Thale**, Ecclesiastic Surgeon; collaborated with Viktor on twenty-seven candidate examinations and privately opposed his final retirement classification.
- **Saint Ilyra of Veyr**, living mercy-domain saint first identified by Viktor at age nine.
- **Tomas Ren**, failed Beatification candidate; disappeared after Viktor recommended immediate removal from family custody and later became one of the earliest suspected private victims.

### VI.2 The Almost-Saints

The Church remembers Father Viktor publicly for the saints he found. The internal record remembers him more accurately for the almost-saints.

Successful saints are easier to archive. They confirm doctrine by existing. The failed candidates leave worse records: children whose families refused removal, adolescent vessels whose fragment contact degraded, adults who had carried angelic pressure for years without formation and had shaped their own lives around it badly, minor miracle-workers who were too frightened or too proud to accept supervision, people whose gifts were real but whose personalities could not bear what had touched them.

Viktor studied these cases obsessively.

His handwriting changes across the decades. Early reports describe failure in pastoral language: *unstable*, *unguarded*, *requires patience*, *not yet ready*, *likely recoverable under formation*. Later reports become colder: *misused capacity*, *refusal pattern established*, *potential no longer supported by discipline*, *candidate in active betrayal of evident calling*.

The word **betrayal** appears first in 2184.

After that, it appears often.

### VI.3 The Formation of the Measuring Mind

No single event made Vaey Viktor into Meruel's vessel. This is important. A single catastrophic revelation would be easier to condemn and easier to excuse.

His alignment formed through repetition.

Every assessment required him to hold two versions of a person in mind: the frightened, partial, compromised person in front of him, and the possible sacred function that person might become if taken, trained, disciplined, and reoriented quickly enough. That comparative act became his vocation. Then it became his instinct. Then it became his prayer.

He stopped asking only whether a candidate was touched by an angel.

He began asking whether they were worthy of the angel that had touched them.

The Rite tolerated this because it produced results. He identified real saints. He prevented several demonic misclassifications. He saw through two politically manufactured miracle cults before they could embarrass the Church. He correctly marked a beloved parish healer as carrying a fragment that was angelic in origin but degrading through self-worship. He was right too often for his superiors to recognize the spiritual deformation hiding inside the accuracy.

By 2190, Sister Aramina Thale's private notes describe him as *"not cruel, but increasingly unable to distinguish mercy from diagnostic contamination."*

No formal action followed.

## VII. THE INDEXING OF MERUEL

### VII.1 First Confirmed Contact

The first confirmed Meruel-contact occurred during the assessment of **Liora Venn**, a seventeen-year-old saint-candidate from the Central Region whose involuntary manifestations had gathered a local following before the Church intervened. The case record remains sealed because Liora survived and later entered protected ecclesiastical custody under a different name.

Viktor's report states that during the third interview he perceived, not merely inferred, the completed form of Liora's possible Beatified state. Witnesses observed a pale geometric ring behind him, visible only in reflection. Liora stopped crying and answered seven questions before he asked them.

He wrote afterward:

> *"For the first time, the distance was not imagined. It stood there. She stood beside herself like a cathedral beside its foundation."*

The Rector-Ascendant supervising the case interpreted the event as an assessor's Crowning. Sister Aramina Thale requested extended monitoring. The request was approved, delayed, reduced, and finally absorbed into ordinary follow-up when Liora's case became politically urgent.

By the time anyone returned to the question, Viktor was already using the sight without distress.

### VII.2 Stabilization

Over the next five years, Meruel's pressure stabilized. The Church did not initially name the fragment. Viktor did. This was procedurally irregular, but the name held under subsequent examination, and no counterdesignation replaced it.

The fusion did not give him spectacle. It refined what he already did.

He could see completed silhouettes. He could sense whether a person's present life had diverged from their possible excellence through misfortune, ignorance, fear, injury, or refusal. He could place pressure upon a candidate's strongest unrealized structure, briefly forcing talent, courage, memory, or sanctity toward expression. In controlled assessment, this was invaluable. A frightened vessel could be shown the shape of what they were becoming. A false candidate could be exposed by the absence of any coherent higher form. A damaged candidate could be distinguished from an empty one.

The Church saw proof.

Viktor saw obligation.

### VII.3 The Doctrinal Error

The error was simple enough for a novice to state and difficult enough that no superior acted quickly when a saint-adjacent assessor began living by it.

Meruel measures potential. Meruel does not measure the whole of justice.

Viktor knew the distinction. He taught it in candidate lectures. His own notes preserve it. He understood, at least intellectually, that Potential without Mercy becomes cruelty, Potential without Guilt becomes misapplied blame, Potential without Harm becomes aesthetic judgment, Potential without Readiness becomes coercion, and Potential without Integrity becomes obsession with appearances of greatness rather than truth.

Understanding did not save him.

He came to believe that wasted potential was not tragedy but blasphemy. Not sadness. Not misfortune. Not even failure. Blasphemy. A refusal to inhabit the shape Heaven had made legible.

This is the doctrine beneath the murders.

## VIII. RETIREMENT AND PROTECTION

### VIII.1 Departure From Active Service

Father Viktor retired from active Obsidian Rite service in 2196 after forty years of assessment work. The official language records voluntary retirement following confirmed Beatified stabilization, with continued advisory availability in exceptional cases.

The official language is accurate as far as it goes.

It does not include the final review meeting in which Mother Calienne Voss argued that his recent case recommendations had become spiritually punitive. It does not include Sister Aramina Thale's objection to allowing an uncontained Potential-domain Beatified to retain private consultative access. It does not include the Arch-Deacon's decision that a public dispute over a confirmed angelic vessel would damage the Rite at a moment when two living saints discovered by Viktor were already under political pressure from their home districts.

The compromise was retirement.

He would no longer hold active assessment authority. He would retain honor, housing, restricted but not unrestricted access, and advisory status. His superiors could say they had removed him from the work. His supporters could say he had been allowed to rest after long service. His critics could say nothing with enough evidence to survive the politics of accusation.

Viktor accepted the decision without visible resentment.

This should have alarmed them more than it did.

### VIII.2 Why He Was Not Stopped

The Church does not stop Father Viktor because stopping him would require a sentence it has not yet found the courage to speak:

A confirmed angelic vessel can become an atrocity without ceasing to be angelic.

There are other reasons. They are smaller but operationally decisive.

He is old. He is honored. He discovered saints. He retains the affection of several living Beatified who believe he saved them from uncontrolled manifestation. His victims are often people already compromised in sealed Church records: failed candidates, withdrawn prodigies, unregulated vessels, apostates, disgraced miracle-workers, or talented individuals who refused formation. Their deaths can be categorized as civil misfortune, private violence, demonic suspicion, disappearance, or politically inconvenient tragedy.

More than once, the institution has benefited from the silence following one of his judgments.

No single office ordered him protected.

No single office has had to.

Protection emerged from hesitation, gratitude, fear, doctrinal pride, and the administrative convenience of not looking directly at what the Church's own saint has become.

## IX. PRESENT ACTIVITY

Father Viktor's current movements are irregular but not hidden. He travels under his own name, stays in Church properties when available, and occasionally accepts invitations to consult on difficult cases where local clergy are divided over whether a manifestation is angelic, demonic, false, or unstable.

He does not kill all those he judges. This has allowed some defenders to preserve the fiction that his severity is controlled.

His known or suspected targets share a pattern:

- gifted individuals who abandoned their gift;
- saint-candidates who resisted Church custody;
- former Redactors or diviners who chose ordinary lives after recognized potential;
- heirs who refused obligations attached to capacity;
- artists, scholars, soldiers, or clergy whose talents were once extraordinary and later deliberately buried;
- people recorded in old assessment files as recoverable who later became, in his word, **discarded by themselves**.

His murders often resemble assessment procedures. He observes. He interviews witnesses. He reconstructs the completed silhouette. He arranges a private meeting. He offers the target a final experience of what they might have been. Then he judges whether the distance between possible self and present self can still be called human weakness rather than spiritual refusal.

If he decides it cannot, he kills.

The deaths vary in method. The signature does not.

At several scenes, investigators or Church intermediaries have recovered narrow white-gold marks drawn in six-part arrangement, with one line completed and five left unfinished. In three cases, a notation was found in Viktor's hand:

> *Potential exceeded present coherence by intolerable margin.*

The civil authorities do not possess a complete comparison file.

The Diaconate does.

## X. DIVINE PROFILE

### X.1 Completed Silhouette

Viktor's primary manifestation is the perception of completed potential. He sees not a future but a possible coherent state: the person as they might have stabilized under sufficient discipline, courage, formation, opportunity, and alignment.

In minor expression, this appears as intuition. He knows where a person's strength has been neglected. He knows whether a candidate is frightened because they are empty or frightened because something immense is pressing from within. He knows which question will cause the real shape to answer.

In full expression, the completed silhouette becomes visible to him as an overlay: taller, clearer, more severe, sometimes beautiful, sometimes unbearable. He has described some silhouettes as *"standing behind the living like an accusation."*

### X.2 Completion Pressure

He can exert Meruel's domain through the vessel, forcing a person briefly toward their completed silhouette. This can restore abandoned skill, sharpen cognition, stabilize courage, reopen a suppressed divine contact, or make a body remember training it has not used in years.

The effect is temporary and cruel in his hands.

A pianist who has not played in twenty years may play flawlessly for forty seconds. A failed priest may speak with the authority they might have carried. A coward may stand upright. A saint-candidate who fled may radiate once, perfectly, and then collapse under the knowledge that they could have done so all along.

Viktor calls this mercy.

It is often the last thing his victims experience before death.

### X.3 Incompletion Wounds

When he harms through Meruel, the damage tends to express symbolically through the failed domain. A singer's throat opens along a line too clean to be a cut. A scholar's dominant hand stiffens around invisible writing before the bones fracture. A former vessel's chest scars in the shape of an unfinished halo. A soldier's spine locks as though held against a standard it cannot meet.

These wounds are not random torture. They are verdicts made anatomical.

### X.4 Self-Measurement

He can perceive his own completed silhouette.

This has not humbled him in any useful way.

He knows he is incomplete. He does not deny it. He argues that his incompletion remains active, disciplined, and moving toward convergence. This is the distinction by which he exempts himself from the category into which he places his victims.

The argument is internally coherent.

That is part of why he remains dangerous.

## XI. SELECTED CASE HISTORY

### XI.1 Saint Ilyra of Veyr

Ilyra was nine years old when Viktor assessed her after a mercy-domain manifestation in a flood clinic. Local clergy believed she had performed healing. Viktor correctly determined that she had not healed the dying so much as held their fear still long enough for their bodies to survive what panic would have worsened.

He recommended immediate removal, protective formation, and no public cult.

The recommendation saved her life.

Saint Ilyra, now twenty-eight, still defends him in private correspondence. She has written that Father Viktor was the first person who looked at her miracle and saw a child inside it.

This is true.

It is also why her defense of him has become difficult to challenge.

### XI.2 Tomas Ren

Tomas Ren was assessed at sixteen after repeated sight-adjacent manifestations and one probable angelic contact. Viktor marked him as a high-potential candidate with unstable discipline and unusually strong resistance to external authority. The family refused removal. The district Arch-Deacon declined to press the matter because the case had become public and politically delicate.

Tomas disappeared at twenty-eight.

No body was recovered. A six-line mark was found burned into the underside of his writing desk. The completed line corresponded to Meruel.

This is the earliest case Mother Calienne Voss believed to be Viktor's work.

### XI.3 Liora Venn

Liora Venn survived. Her case matters because Meruel first stabilized through Viktor during her assessment.

She remains under protected name and does not receive visitors without Diaconate authorization. Her current file records recurring nightmares of Father Viktor standing beside a version of her made of white architecture. She does not accuse him of harming her.

She has refused to be in the same building with him since 2194.

### XI.4 The Aldhor Scholar

Marek Aldhor, a historian of minor House descent, abandoned a promising theological reconstruction project after family pressure and accepted a comfortable administrative post. Viktor had no formal jurisdiction over him. Marek was found dead in his private study with thirty-two pages of his unfinished manuscript arranged around the body.

The last page bore a sentence in Viktor's hand:

> *He knew where the door was and called the wall merciful.*

The Church attributed the death to a private dispute. The family accepted the explanation because the alternative would have attached scandal to both the House and the Church.

### XI.5 Sister Ema Thorne

Ema Thorne was a former candidate for Obsidian investiture who withdrew after a failed Crowning left her with chronic pain and aversion to liturgical contact. Viktor visited her twice after retirement. The third visit was unrecorded.

She was found alive.

This remains the most useful surviving witness account. She reports that Viktor placed Meruel's pressure upon her and showed her the Votary she might have become. She could not sustain the state. He wept. Then he apologized and left.

No one knows why he spared her.

She believes he saw that her refusal was not refusal.

She also believes he will eventually decide he was wrong.

## XII. WHAT HE BELIEVES

Father Viktor's theology can be summarized in a sentence:

> Greatness is not a privilege. It is a debt.

He does not hate weakness. This point must be preserved because mischaracterizing him produces bad predictions. He can be gentle toward ordinary people. He respects honest smallness. He has no contempt for the person who possessed no extraordinary capacity and lived within the limits of their own measure.

His hatred is reserved for those who had more.

In his private writings, he divides human failure into three categories:

1. **Insufficiency** - the person did not have the structure required. This is not sin.
2. **Injury** - the person was prevented, damaged, delayed, or broken. This may be tragic and may remain recoverable.
3. **Refusal** - the person saw, knew, or was given enough to become greater and chose comfort, resentment, cowardice, vanity, or ordinary happiness instead.

Only the third category reliably receives his verdict.

The horror lies in the fact that Meruel gives him real information and incomplete wisdom. He can often tell the difference between insufficiency and refusal. He cannot reliably tell whether the refusal should be answered with patience, correction, grief, or restraint.

He supplies death as the answer.

## XIII. WHAT HE DOES NOT KNOW, OR WILL NOT ACCEPT

He does not know, or will not accept:

- that Meruel is not the whole of justice;
- that potential can be real without being obligatory in the way he believes;
- that peace is not always cowardice;
- that a person may choose love, obscurity, survival, or rest without desecrating the gift they carried;
- that some failures are still alive in the direction of recovery;
- that the Church's failure to stop him is not confirmation of his holiness;
- that being protected by frightened institutions is not the same thing as being vindicated by Heaven;
- that his own self-measurement is compromised by the need to remain the judge rather than become the judged.

Most dangerously, he does not accept that mercy can know things potential cannot.

## XIV. CURRENT ASSESSMENT

Father Vaey Viktor is not a fallen priest in the simple sense. That category would be more convenient. He has not renounced the Sovereign. He has not embraced demonic pressure. He has not fabricated his sanctity. He remains, by every structural test available to the Church, a genuine vessel of an angelic fragment.

This is precisely why the case is intolerable.

He represents a failure in the Church's own distinction between holiness and governance. The institution knows how to identify demonic corruption. It knows how to condemn false miracle-workers. It knows how to pity broken vessels and how to venerate saints whose lives make doctrine beautiful. It has far less courage when confronted with a saint whose miracles are real, whose fragment is angelic, whose service record contains genuine good, and whose present acts are murder.

He is what happens when assessment becomes worship, when worship becomes measurement, and when measurement forgets that a living person is not a failed version of an imagined one.

He should be contained.

He will probably be consulted again before he is stopped.

That sentence is the shame of this file.
