## What It Is

The Stase is a region on Terra's Dark Side where **normal traversal of space and time is suspended**. It is not a geographic zone in the ordinary sense. It does not have clean boundaries that can be marked on a map, because the condition that defines it is not positional but structural — a disruption in how the underlying mechanics of reality function in that area, producing effects that include temporal inconsistency, spatial displacement, and the persistence of things that should not persist.

It was not always there. It was created.

---

## Origins: A Precursor Experiment Gone Wrong

The Stase is a Precursor experimental failure. The precise nature of the experiment is not in any record available to current institutions. What the evidence indicates is this: the Precursors conducted research in the Dark Side that involved deliberately altering how traversal mechanics functioned in a bounded region. The experiment proceeded to a point. Then it went wrong in a way they were not able to simply reverse.

The result was a zone where the mechanics of traversal remained altered permanently — not in the bounded, controlled way the experiment intended, but in an ongoing, self-sustaining way that the Precursors' subsequent remediation efforts did not fully resolve.

The Stase has been there, in roughly its current configuration, for a very long time. Possibly since before the Fracture. The Fracture's effects on the Stase — whether the threshold compromise made it worse, or simply made what was always there more accessible to what lies outside the threshold — is a question the Keepers of Before hold an opinion on that they have not published.

## What Lives There

The Stase produced two categories of entity that represent distinct problems.

**Stase Residuals** — referred to colloquially as **Dark Ends** in military doctrine — are what happens when a biological organism enters the Stase zone and becomes caught in the traversal suspension. The traversal mechanics that normally govern biological process — growth, death, decay, the linear progression of time through a living system — stop functioning normally. The organism does not die in the way it would outside. It persists in a state that is not life in any meaningful sense but is not death either. It is very dangerous. It does not behave in ways that normal biological threat assessment predicts. Dark Ends are the primary adversarial element in the War of the Threshold's northern front engagements.

**Traversal-Independent Entities** — **SOEs**, in StaSis and Obsidian Rite classification — are a different problem. These are entities that do not originate from biological organisms caught in the Stase. They are native to the altered traversal environment, or they arrived through it from wherever the threshold compromise allowed access to. They are categorically unlike Dark Ends. They do not have the residual biological signatures of Stase-altered organisms. Their behavior is not explicable through altered-biology frameworks. What they are, where they are from, and what they want are questions that StaSis tracks in a catalogue that is not public.

The practical distinction matters operationally: Obsidian Rite doctrine and northern front military doctrine are both calibrated for Dark Ends as the primary threat category. SOE engagement requires different doctrine, different resources, and different authorization — and happens at frequencies the public record significantly underreports.

## The War of the Threshold

The **War of the Threshold** is the Empire's ongoing military campaign at the Darkmargin — the frontier zone where the Twilight Band's edge meets the permanent dark. It has been ongoing for a long time. Long enough that most citizens know it as a background fact of the Empire rather than an active crisis — something that requires soldiers and Church field orders and occasional major engagements, but which has been contained at the frontier for generations.

This understanding is accurate in a narrow operational sense: the frontier has not moved dramatically in living memory. It is not accurate in the sense that implies the situation is stable or resolved. The War of the Threshold is the world's method of managing what the Stase produces and what the Fracture's threshold compromise allows. It is not a war that can be won. It is a war that can be maintained.

The Church's field operations at the northern front exist because Dark Ends and SOEs at the higher end of the classification taxonomy require intervention tools that military doctrine alone does not provide. The Obsidian Rite's presence in the theatre is not ceremonial support. It is operational necessity, acknowledged in treaty language.

## Classification and Institutional Knowledge

The Stase is not publicly named as a Precursor experimental failure in any document available through standard institutional channels. The military has a working knowledge of what emerges from it — Dark Ends, the specific engagement challenges they present, the maps of where the Stase boundary approximately runs — without having the deeper history of why it is there.

**StaSis** (SiNE's anomalous entity division, designation S-AE) maintains the most complete living catalogue of SOE activity and characteristics. Their classification system for Traversal-Independent Entities is the most sophisticated in current use. How they developed it, and what proportion of it came from engagement records versus other intelligence sources, is not in their public documentation.

The Church holds what it holds in restricted theological record, under the Keepers of Before's custody. Their understanding of the Stase's relationship to the Fracture and the threshold compromise is more complete than the Apocachynthion's, and their practical engagement doctrine — built from the Obsidian Rite's accumulated field experience — is correspondingly more sophisticated at the high end of the threat taxonomy.

None of the institutions that have pieces of this picture have yet assembled them into a single coherent account. Whether this is deliberate, structural, or simply the result of institutional boundaries and information compartmentalization is a question each institution would answer differently.
