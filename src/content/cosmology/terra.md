## The World

Terra is not a natural planet. It is a Precursor engineering project — a laboratory environment designed and stabilized by an ancient civilization that operated at scales of time and power that most current institutions lack the framework to comprehend.

Its circumference is approximately 80,000 kilometres. It orbits **Vael'Khar**, a supermassive black hole, rather than a conventional star. The light and heat that reach its surface come from the accretion disk surrounding Vael'Khar — a permanent radiant source that does not move across the sky, does not set, and does not follow the diurnal patterns most habitable worlds take for granted.

As a result, Terra is **tidally locked**. One hemisphere faces Vael'Khar permanently. The other faces away into permanent dark. Neither of these hemispheres is hospitable to life in any sustained way.

---

## The Twilight Band

Between the bright hemisphere and the dark lies a band roughly two thousand kilometres wide where life is possible. This is the **Twilight Band** — the ring civilization that all known history has unfolded within.

The Twilight Band is not a single climate. It varies considerably across its length and the ring's shifting orientation relative to the accretion disk, producing what researchers divide into **six distinct radiation seasons** — not driven by axial tilt or orbital period in the conventional sense, but by the angular relationship between Terra's orbital position around Vael'Khar and the disc's output geometry. Some seasons bring more intense radiant energy from the disc to the band's inner edge; others ease it. The variation is predictable enough for agriculture and enough for seasons. It is not predictable enough to ignore.

The Band's inner edge — the **Terminal Zones** — experiences the heaviest disc-side radiation and hosts more arid, high-energy environments. The outer edge transitions gradually toward the cold and dark of the **Darkmargin**, where the band's organized climate gives way to polar night. The center, the **Central Twilight Corridor**, is where most of the Empire's population and infrastructure concentrates.

## Why the World Works

The Twilight Band's stability is not natural in any meaningful sense. Maintaining a habitable atmosphere on a tidally locked world in this orbital configuration requires active management of factors that unaided planetary physics would not resolve favorably.

In the earliest era of the world, this management was performed directly by the Precursors and their instruments. **Aesis**, the Skyphon whose Jurisdiction covered atmospheric coherence, held the conditions of the Band in function through her direct presence in them — not engineering the atmosphere from outside but inhabiting its dynamics, correcting drift before it became instability.

After the Fracture ended that era, the Precursors' remaining infrastructure took over: a distributed network of stabilization systems embedded in Terra's structure, maintaining what the Instruments once governed through active presence. These systems have operated for the entire span of recorded history and for an indeterminate period before it. The civilizations that use the world do not generally know they exist. They do not need to. The systems continue regardless.

## The Orbital Position and the Callings

Terra's orbit around Vael'Khar is not a fixed circle. It varies. When Terra's orbit brings it to **periapsis** — its closest approach to Vael'Khar — the gravitational and energetic conditions affecting the Lunar Crown intensify. This is when the **Callings** are at their strongest.

The Callings are resonance surges originating from the fractured Lunar Crown, reflecting the containment conflict ongoing within it. Periapsis conditions do not cause them — the conflict in the Crown is continuous — but they amplify them. The correlation is well-documented by the Apocachynthion's monitoring infrastructure, though its deeper explanation remains institutionally contested.

## The Dark Side

Beyond the Twilight Band, on the hemisphere that faces permanently away from Vael'Khar, lies the **Dark Side**. It is cold, permanently lightless, and in large measure unexplored in any sustained way.

The Dark Side is not simply empty. Along its boundary with the Twilight Band — the **Darkmargin** — the Empire maintains a long military campaign against things that emerge from deeper within it. What those things are, where they originate, and how the Dark Side became what it has become is a matter the Church holds in restricted theological record and the military holds in operational necessity.

The short version that most citizens know: the Dark Side is where the bad things come from, and the northern frontier is where the Empire tries to stop them.

## What the World Was Built For

The Precursors built Terra as a **laboratory Page** — a world-scale environment for the study of conditions they were interested in. The civilization that grew up in the Twilight Band was not incidental to this. Biological and social complexity was part of what the Precursors were studying, in ways that current institutions are not positioned to fully appreciate.

This history does not invalidate any aspect of life on Terra. People are born, live, and die in the Twilight Band without reference to who made the world or why. The fact that the world was engineered does not make the sunsets less real. It does not make the grief of losing someone less real. The Precursors made a world. What grew on it was its own.

What it does affect is how some very old, very careful observers — the Keepers of Before, certain Precursor-adjacent figures who have been watching longer than any institution has existed — think about where this particular Page is headed.
