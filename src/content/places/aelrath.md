## The Threshold Port

Ael'rath is the northernmost major city of the Empire and the primary naval base of the **Cold Fleet**. It sits at the base of a sea cliff on the Dark-Side coast, partially carved into the rock of the cliff face itself — a construction technique developed over generations to protect against the wind events that drive in off the frozen outer ocean.

The harbor is an engineering achievement: a series of reinforced basins cut into the cliff base, sheltered from the worst ice conditions by a curved breakwater of black stone that took over a century to complete. Cold Fleet vessels dock in these protected basins between frontier patrols, their hulls scarred by dark-water service and the particular stresses that Stase-adjacent operation places on ship materials.

The city is **dark, close, and perpetually cold**. Its population is composed largely of Navy personnel, Night Legion support elements, fishing communities, and the specialist traders who supply both. The sky above Ael'rath is rarely more than dim twilight even at the best orbital positions. The accretion disk's light reaches this far north at too shallow an angle to warm anything.

Population: approximately **400,000**.

## The Black Shore

The **Black Shore** runs directly north of the harbor — fine black sand composed of dense basaltic material, broken down over millennia by wave action, glacial melt, and the persistent rain and ice cycles of the northern coast. The sand appears to absorb available light rather than reflect it. On overcast nights, the beach is nearly indistinguishable from the dark water's edge.

Within northern culture, the Black Shores carry layered significance. Fishermen read the texture of the sand for weather patterns. Military tradition holds that a soldier who has crossed the Black Shore toward the Threshold has entered a different kind of existence — one measured in endurance rather than years. The Threshold's dark beaches are where Night Legion recruits complete their final conditioning before assignment to the war.

## The Salt Flats of Carendis

South of Ael'rath, where a wide coastal plain meets a shallow inland sea, lies the **Carendis Salt Flat complex** — a vast expanse of mineral-crusted terrain approximately 3,000 square kilometers in area, formed over geological time by evaporation cycles of shallow coastal waters interacting with mineral-rich glacial runoff from the highlands above.

The flats are blindingly reflective during any period of reasonable lunar illumination. The surface is hard in some areas and deceptively soft in others, prone to sudden collapse over subsurface brine pockets. The mineral deposits are commercially valuable and harvested seasonally by specialist operations out of Ael'rath.

The flats' primary military significance is as a **natural barrier**: they channel ground movement between the coastal zone and the highland approaches into defined corridors, making the terrain around Ael'rath more defensible than a coastal city of its size would otherwise be.

The way the lunar light reflects across the white crust at night — a cold, flat, silver expanse under the dim sky — is a common subject in northern literature and visual art. Ael'rath's sailors use the flats' reflective surface as a navigation landmark during clear-sky periods.

## Endurance

Three significant Dark End incursions have reached Ael'rath over the city's history. Each time, the cliffs held, the harbor held, and the city held. This record is not taken for granted by the people who live there. It is taken as the specific kind of proof that a northern city provides: not that the war can be won, but that the city can survive it, and that surviving it is sufficient.
