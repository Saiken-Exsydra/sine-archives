## The Timber City

Kor'rath occupies a wide highland river valley deep in the northern forested region, approximately equidistant between the Dark-Side coast and the Aurora border. It is the center of the Empire's **timber industry** — a city whose entire economic identity is organized around the extraction, processing, and distribution of the northern conifer forest.

The forests surrounding Kor'rath are among the largest continuous forested areas in the Empire: vast stretches of tall, densely packed conifers with dark bark and deep green canopy, thriving in the cold, rain-soaked conditions of the Northern Region. Sawmills line the river for several kilometers. The sound of blade on timber and the splash of logs entering the water are as constant as the rain.

Beyond timber, Kor'rath is also a **regional market hub** for the smaller communities scattered across the northern highland. The city serves the highland settlements the way a trading capital serves its hinterland — as the place where goods converge, contracts are made, and information from the wider Empire filters in.

Population: approximately **800,000**.

## The Seasons

Kor'rath's character changes dramatically between the radiation seasons. During the **High Radiance season**, it is one of the busiest trade cities in the north — its river port handling significant cargo volume, the highland roads open and passable, the market districts crowded with traders from across the region and occasionally from the Central Region.

During **deep Low Radiance**, the river slows. The highland roads become difficult, then dangerous, then impassable for heavy freight. Kor'rath turns inward — a quiet, firelit city under heavy snow, its population living on the stockpiles accumulated during the productive months, its taverns and meeting halls serving as the social infrastructure that holds a snowbound community together. This seasonal rhythm is not hardship. It is the texture of northern life, and Kor'rath has been living it for longer than most cities in the Empire have existed.
