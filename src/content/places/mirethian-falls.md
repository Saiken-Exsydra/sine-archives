## The Falls

Where the largest highland tributary of the **Velz'kaya River** drops from the edge of the Hal'vorn Ridge System into the lowland valley below, it produces one of the most spectacular natural features in the Empire — the Mire'thian Falls. The primary cascade drops approximately **350 meters** in a single unbroken column before fragmenting into a wide spray basin at the valley floor.

The falls are permanent year-round, fed by snowmelt and rainfall across a vast highland catchment. The volume varies with the seasons but never ceases — a continuous column of water falling from the ridge edge into the valley below, producing a roar that is audible for several kilometers during peak flow.

---

## The Seasons

In **Low Radiance season**, the reduced temperatures transform the falls' environment. The mist freezes into elaborate ice formations along the surrounding cliffs — layered ice curtains, crystalline accumulations, and frozen spray structures that attract considerable numbers of visitors from Hal'vorn and the surrounding settlements. The falls themselves continue beneath the ice, their flow sustained by the highland snowpack.

In **High Radiance season**, the increased melt volume amplifies the falls to their maximum — a wider, louder, more violent cascade that fills the spray basin and sends moisture into the air for hundreds of meters downstream. The surrounding forest is at its greenest and densest during this period, the conifer canopy deepened by the combination of rain, melt, and the falls' own contribution to local humidity.

## Mireth

The town of **Mireth** has grown at the base of the falls over generations — initially a waypoint settlement, now a permanent community of roughly **80,000** that sustains itself on the timber trade, the falls' hydroelectric generation system, and the steady flow of visitors from across the Northern Region and beyond.

Mireth is not directly accessible by the Imperial Spine but is served by a spur line from Hal'vorn. The town's character is shaped by the falls' constant presence: the sound, the moisture in the air, the particular quality of light as it refracts through the spray. Residents describe the falls as something they stop hearing after a few months — a background that becomes silence — and visitors describe the silence when they leave as the strangest part of returning home.
