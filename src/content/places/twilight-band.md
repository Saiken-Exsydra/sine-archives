## The Band

The Twilight Band is the ring of habitable territory on Terra's surface where Vael'Khar's accretion disk provides sufficient light and heat to sustain the biosphere without exceeding the temperature ranges that biological systems can tolerate.

Terra is tidally locked — one face pointed permanently at Vael'Khar, the other permanently away. The face toward Vael'Khar receives continuous accretion disk radiation, which produces temperatures and energy levels incompatible with sustained life in any form the biosphere has produced. The face away from Vael'Khar receives nothing from the disc, and exists at temperatures approaching the background of space.

Between these two extremes is the Band: approximately two thousand kilometres of territory, eighty thousand kilometres around, where the disc's light arrives at angles and intensities that the biosphere — designed, in the Precursor framework, to function under exactly these conditions — can inhabit.

This is where everyone lives. This is where everything has ever happened.

## Internal Structure

The Band is not a single climate. Its interior varies considerably in the directions that matter.

**Terminal Zones** are the Band's inner edge — the territories closest to the disc-facing hemisphere. Here the accretion disk radiation arrives most directly, producing the hottest and most energetically intense environments in the Band. Agriculture is possible in the Terminal Zones but requires varieties adapted to the high-radiation conditions. The Terminal Zone aesthetic — architecture designed for heat management, populations concentrated in protected valleys, the specific quality of light from a perpetual disc-glare — is distinctive within the Band's broader geographic culture.

**The Central Twilight Corridor** is the Band's middle region, where conditions are most favorable for the full range of human settlement and agriculture. The majority of the Empire's population lives in the Corridor. The Empire's major cities — Kalt'sen, Vel'hara, the academy cities, the commercial hubs — are Corridor settlements. The climate here is the most varied and the most comfortable: warm enough for the biosphere to be productive, shielded enough from the disc to avoid the Terminal Zones' energy intensity, and far enough from the Darkmargin to be outside the permanent chill that comes off the dark hemisphere.

**The Outer Band** is the territory between the Corridor and the **Darkmargin** — the zone where habitable conditions thin and the permanent cold of the dark hemisphere begins to intrude. The Outer Band is inhabited but sparsely. Its cultures are shaped by the cold and by proximity to the frontier; the emotional and cultural texture of Outer Band settlements is different from Corridor settlements in ways that travellers notice and that residents have ceased to notice.

## The Radiation Seasons

The Band does not experience seasons driven by axial tilt in the conventional planetary sense. What it experiences instead is a **six-season cycle** driven by the relationship between Terra's orbital position around Vael'Khar and the geometry of the accretion disk's output.

As Terra's orbit carries it through different positions relative to Vael'Khar, the angle and intensity at which disc radiation reaches the Band's inner edge varies. This produces six recognizable seasonal conditions — periods of higher and lower radiation intensity, of different spectrum compositions in the disc light, of varying energetic input to the biosphere and the BrightCrystal infrastructure that runs on it.

The seasons are named and tracked. They are relevant to agriculture, to BrightCrystal processing operations whose efficiency varies with radiation input, and to the Calling calendar — because Terra's orbital position also determines the intensity of resonance surges from the Lunar Crown, and Calling events are strongest near periapsis.

## The Darkmargin

The northern edge of the Band — where "north" in the Band's geography means "toward the dark hemisphere" rather than toward a geographic pole — is the **Darkmargin**: the coastal frontier zone where habitable conditions end and the permanent dark begins.

The waters of the Darkmargin are cold, dark, and permanently so. The light from the disc reaches them at too shallow an angle to warm them, and the darkness that begins a few kilometres offshore is the darkness of a hemisphere that faces away from all available light sources.

The Darkmargin is where the War of the Threshold is fought. It is where what emerges from the Stase zone on the Dark Side approaches the Band's occupied territory, where HourGlass's Cold Fleet operates, and where the Victorian Rite maintains its sustained presence. It is a frontier in both the geographic and the psychological sense: the edge of the world that everyone knows about and most citizens of the Corridor have no direct experience of.

Settlements along the Darkmargin are structured around the frontier's demands: fortified, logistically connected to the military presence, economically shaped by the Cold Fleet's need for supply and the Obsidian Rite's need for support infrastructure. The people who live there know the war in a way that interior citizens do not. The war is not abstract. It is the sound of HourGlass vessels returning to port in the early dark hours, the particular quality of attention that the local Church parish maintains, the specific kind of help that the Menders provide in communities where survivors of Dark Side contact are not rare.

## Scale and Distances

Eighty thousand kilometres is, by any experiential measure, a large number. The Band's geography is not a single comprehensible territory in the way a country is. It is a ring civilization — an inhabited ring whose internal distances are such that the cultures at opposite points have more in common with the local geography and climate than with each other.

Travel across the Band is organized around the BrightCrystal-powered transit infrastructure that the Empire has developed and maintained. This infrastructure makes the distances manageable in a practical sense: transit times that would otherwise be measured in weeks are measured in days for most destinations. But the distances remain real. The communities at one edge of the Band and the communities at the other edge are both inside the Twilight Band, both under the Empire's governance, and genuinely different places with genuinely different cultures, histories, and local concerns.

This is one of the Empire's sustained governance challenges. The Apocachynthion manages it by distributing academies across the Band rather than centralizing all Redactory training in Kalt'sen. The Church manages it through a parish network that is geographically exhaustive rather than concentrated. SiNE manages it through regional command structures for HourGlass and distributed operational presence for StaSis. The scale of the ring is a fact that every institution operating across all of it has had to adapt its structure to accommodate.
