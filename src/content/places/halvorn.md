## The Northern Capital

Hal'vorn is the administrative capital of the Northern Region, positioned in the upper river valley where the largest of the northern highland rivers descends from the ridge country and broadens into navigable depth. The city is built against a hillside — its older districts climbing the slope in dense, narrow-streeted layers while the modern port and industrial quarter occupy the flat valley floor beside the river.

It is a city of **stone and timber**. The highland quarries provide the stone, and the vast northern conifer forests provide timber in quantities unmatched anywhere else in the Empire. The architecture is heavier and more compressed than Kalt'sen's — adapted to heavier snowfall and colder temperatures. Buildings here are built to hold heat and shed snow. The streets are narrow in the old quarters because narrow streets are warmer streets when the wind comes down from the ridgeline.

The harbor is active year-round, protected from ice by both natural geography and the modest geothermal warming of the river's lower reach. It handles the timber trade, the military supply chain for the Threshold frontier, and the general commerce of the Northern Region's highland communities.

Population: approximately **1.5 to 2 million**.

## The Night Legion Academies

Above the city, occupying a plateau ridge with commanding views of the valley and the highland approaches, sit the principal **Night Legion training academies** — walled complexes where the soldiers who fight the War of the Threshold are prepared for what that war requires.

The academies are visible from the city below. Their presence gives Hal'vorn a specific character that other regional capitals do not share: the awareness that the war is not abstract here. The people who will fight it are training on the ridge above the market district. The people who have fought it are drinking in the taverns below.

## The Hal'vorn Ridge System

The city gives its name to the **Hal'vorn Ridge System** — a series of ancient glaciated ridgelines running roughly east-west across the upper Northern Region, forming the natural northern spine of the Empire's defensive geography against Aurora. The ridges are not impossibly tall, but they are continuous, heavily forested on their southern faces, and laced with narrow river-cut passes that funnel any large-scale movement into predictable corridors.

The **Greygate Pass**, east of Hal'vorn, is the primary legitimate land crossing between Aurora and the Empire — permanently staffed by an imperial garrison and the primary legal crossing point for trade and diplomatic transit. Two smaller passes to the west are monitored by highland stations rather than permanent garrisons.

## The Northern Character

Hal'vorn carries the cultural weight of the north — the austerity, the endurance, the cold-tempered character that northern people regard as self-evident virtue and that central and southern citizens sometimes regard as performative grimness. The city is closer to the War of the Threshold than any other regional capital. The Cold Fleet's supply chain runs through its harbor. The Night Legion's institutional heart beats on its ridge.

During the **High Radiance season**, Hal'vorn is one of the busiest cities in the north — its river port handling significant cargo volume, the highland roads open, the market districts full. During **deep Low Radiance**, the river slows, the highland roads become difficult, and the city turns inward — stone and timber holding against the snow, firelight in narrow streets, and the particular quiet of a northern city that has been doing this for longer than most southern cities have existed.
