## The Caldera

The Vael Mouth is the most dramatic geological feature in the Empire — a **collapsed volcanic caldera system approximately 85 km wide**, located within the Mid Scorch Belt. On clear High Radiance days, it is visible as a circular depression of darker rock against the pale plateau surface — a wound in the landscape that has not healed and shows no intention of healing.

The caldera is not volcanically active in the conventional sense. Instead, it vents continuously from dozens of **geothermal fissures** in its floor, producing persistent columns of steam and heat-shimmer that are visible from significant distances. The air above the Vael Mouth carries the mineral tang of deep-earth venting and the particular quality of heat that comes from geological processes operating at pressures the surface world does not normally encounter.

---

## The Geology

Within the caldera walls, the **geological compression from Vael'Khar's curvature gradient** is measurably more intense than in the surrounding region. The curvature-induced shear stress that produces BrightCrystal formation across the southern belts is concentrated here — focused by the caldera's geometry into conditions that push mineral formation to its extremes.

BrightCrystal formations in the caldera walls are among the **densest naturally occurring deposits** known. The crystal faces are visible in exposed cliff sections — lattice structures of extraordinary density and purity, the kind of material that the Empire's energy and military infrastructure ultimately depends on, occurring here in concentrations that would make any extraction engineer's career if they could be safely reached.

They cannot. The heat venting, structural instability, and thinness levels within the Vael Mouth make it one of the most hazardous environments in the Empire. Multiple extraction attempts over the centuries have been abandoned due to seismic events and structural emergencies. The Extraction Commission classifies it as a **Category One Hazard Zone**.

## The Name

The name is an informal reference to Vael'Khar — the black hole around which Terra orbits. Most who use the name do not understand the geological mechanism that makes the connection accurate: the caldera's extreme conditions are a direct product of Vael'Khar's gravitational gradient acting on the planet's crust with particular intensity at this point. The Vael Mouth is, in a real geological sense, a place where the relationship between Terra and the force that governs it is most physically manifest.

Within popular culture and folk tradition, the Vael Mouth is considered a sacred or cursed site depending on the tradition — a point where the world breathes, where the deep earth speaks, where the boundary between what can be understood and what simply is becomes thinner than it is anywhere else in the daylight world.
