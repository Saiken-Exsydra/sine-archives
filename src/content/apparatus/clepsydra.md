## Physical Description

Clepsydra is a polearm of unusual construction, approximately two metres and forty centimetres from tip to tip, with a separation joint located precisely at its midpoint. Both halves are of identical weight despite the dissimilarity of their blades. The forward element terminates in a spear-blade: leaf-shaped, double-edged, with a profile that tapers to a point of implausible fineness without any observed deformation. The rearward element terminates in a single-edged short blade seated in a swept guard — functional for close work, balanced for one-handed use.

The shaft is a dense, faintly warm white. It is not cold to the touch as metal typically is. Several assessors over the years have described it as **present** in a way that a tool is not — the sensation of holding something that is aware of being held, rather than something inert in a hand.

When joined, the weapon's balance is exceptional. When divided, each half maintains independent balance as though engineered for single-handed use from the beginning. The separation joint leaves no visible seam when closed.

The iridescence of the surface is subtle enough to miss in direct radiance. It appears primarily in peripheral vision and during moonset, when ambient silver-white light strikes the weapon at oblique angles. Observers who have held it through a full lunar cycle report the iridescence intensifying incrementally during the dark portion of the cycle.

The inscription visible on the interior face of the lower blade housing cannot be removed. It has not responded to Redactory overlay. Its script is pre-fracture in structural character but cannot be matched to any Precursor inscription catalogued in the Archive.

## Material Composition

The alloy is unidentified — white with a faint iridescence that does not correspond to any known BrightCrystal refraction index. The surface does not accept damage by any tested means short of Archtype-level Redactory pressure, and damage inflicted at that threshold heals within approximately twelve hours of ceasing contact.

## Origin and History

### The Skyphon of Reality

The Instrument belongs, in its first recorded provenance, to the Skyphon designated in recovered mythology and Church restricted archive alike as the **Skyphon of Reality** — the First Bearer assigned to carry this particular Instrument through the world.

The Skyphons were the First Bearers: neither mortal nor instrument, fashioned to serve as a living interface between the Instruments of the Intact Seal and the world those Instruments were made to protect. The Church holds them in its category of *the Rightly Unmade* — creatures whose dissolution when the Instruments were scattered was not punishment but conclusion.

The Skyphon of Reality was, by the logic of its designation, the Bearer whose specific function concerned the boundary between what exists and what does not. The Instrument it carried reflects this in the principle inscribed within it — *Sôðcwide*, a term appearing in no other pre-fracture document, translated as *The Nothingness*, though a persistent minority of scholars prefers *The Condition of Having Not Yet Been.*

### The Threshold Guardian Function

Recovered notation and fragmentary myth converge on a single consistent claim: Clepsydra was fashioned to guard the entrance to the *first superior dimension* — a term appearing across at least eleven independent textual traditions, with no consensus on what it denotes.

Scholar interpretation splits broadly into two camps. The rationalist tradition within the Index Theorem framework reads *first superior dimension* as a structural analogy for extreme Dive depth. The theological tradition within the Church reads the term literally — a bounded region of the world's architecture that exists above the plane of mortal experience, sealed at the Fracture, and still awaiting the conditions under which it may be approached.

Both traditions agree on one thing: whatever the first superior dimension is, *Clepsydra is the key that refuses to be held by the wrong hand*.

### Post-Fracture History

The history of Clepsydra following the Fracture is, in the language of this office, *a sequence of absences punctuated by catastrophes*.

It does not stay where it is placed. Twelve documented custody records across the Deep Archive's accessible history describe the Instrument as present in a location and then absent — not stolen, not lost, simply *no longer there*. In four of those records, the Instrument subsequently appeared in the possession of someone who had not sought it and, in several cases, had not known it existed. These individuals are logged as *spontaneous users* and their outcomes vary: two died within months; three achieved documented feats of Redactory-adjacent power the Apocachynthion cannot classify; one disappeared; one survived with no reported effects.

The instrument has never been successfully contained.

## The Five Oaths

The sealing of Clepsydra is not metaphorical. It is structural.

The Instrument operates at diminished capacity in the hands of a user who has not fulfilled each of the Five Oaths in sequence. *Diminished* in this context remains considerable — even sealed, the weapon's physical properties exceed all known forged materials, and its capacity to break through Redactory overlay is not limited by the Oaths. What the Oaths gate is access to the Instrument's full capability, which no confirmed user in accessible history has unlocked.

The Oaths are not formulated instructions. They are understood by those who carry it — gradually, in pieces, in the manner of something that cannot be taught but can be arrived at. Assessors who have handled the weapon report a sense of *criteria they do not yet meet*, without being able to name what the criteria are.

**The First Oath** concerns the nature of *Sôðcwide* — the principle inscribed within the Instrument. The bearer must not merely know the word but must have confronted, genuinely and at depth, the question the inscription poses: *why is there something rather than nothing?* The Instrument appears to distinguish between the question asked as inquiry and the question *lived as crisis*.

**The Second through Fifth Oaths** are not documented with equivalent confidence. Fragmentary accounts suggest they proceed through conditions of escalating structural intimacy with the principle. The Fifth Oath, appearing only in two recovered texts, is described as requiring the bearer to *hold nothing as something and not flinch at the weight of it*.

## Selection Behaviour

Clepsydra is not neutral. It has a preference. The Instrument does not respond equally to all who carry it. It does not respond at all to some.

There is one account, filed in 1089 under anomalous Redactory incidents, of an Archtype-class Redactor attempting to compel the Instrument's use through sustained Depth pressure. The Instrument allowed the contact for approximately four minutes. At the end of that period, the Archtype released it and did not attempt to take it up again. A field observer present noted that the Archtype appeared, for the first time in her experience of him, to be *uncertain*.

The Instrument's capacity to reach its user across any distance is consistent across independent accounts. It has been separated from chosen users by physical barriers, containment structures, and on two occasions by Redactory overlay strong enough to seal a moderate-sized room. In every case, the Instrument arrived at the user.

## Known Capabilities

The following capabilities are drawn from observation records, field reports, and recovered historical accounts, and describe the Instrument in its *sealed* state.

### Structural Penetration

Clepsydra passes through Redactory overlay without triggering standard resistance responses. It does not *break* overlay so much as *decline to be stopped by it*. Objects and structures that have been heavily reinforced through Redactory means have been cut by the Instrument without the expected deformation of reinforced material.

### Persistence of Effect

Wounds inflicted by Clepsydra do not respond normally to Redactory overlay aimed at accelerating tissue reconfiguration. Several high-competency Redactors have noted that attempting to close a Clepsydra wound through standard configuration routing produces a sensation of *nothing for the routing to push against* — as though the condition the wound represents has achieved a kind of local structural finality.

### User Arrival

The Instrument arrives at its chosen user regardless of intervening obstacle. This capability does not appear to strain the Instrument. It does not appear to strain the intervening obstacles. It simply occurs.

### Presence in the Dive

Redactors who have encountered the Instrument during Dive states report its presence as structurally *visible* in a way that ordinary objects are not. Two assessors have described it as appearing to have a Depth signature without having a Dive — as though it were routing without routing, stable at a density that living Redactors reach only through sustained effort.

## Institutional Position

The Apocachynthion does not have custody of Clepsydra. The Deep Archive contains eleven formal acquisition efforts across the institution's history. None succeeded. In nine cases, the Instrument was gone before collection could occur. In two cases, the retrieval team declined to take it after contact — both teams filed reports containing the phrase *it was not ready to be taken*, or a variation of it.

The Church's position is that the Obscurials belong to no mortal institution. The Church does not actively pursue the Instrument. It notes the Instrument's existence in its restricted pastoral correspondence and waits.

The Apocachynthion's current operational guidance, written by the Council of Volumes in 1091 and not revised since: *if a confirmed user appears in possession of Clepsydra and is willing to engage with the institution, that engagement should be facilitated at the highest appropriate level of institutional attention. The Instrument cannot be assigned. It cannot be regulated. What can be done is to provide whatever support the chosen user requires and document everything that occurs in their presence.*

## Theoretical Annotation — On the Principle of Sôðcwide

*Contributed by Senior Assessor Vel'kara, Stylus-rank. Filed 1104.*

---

The principle inscribed on this Instrument — *Sôðcwide*, The Nothingness — is not a philosophical position in any tradition I recognise. It is a **structural claim**.

Every Redactor who has held this weapon and Dived in its presence has reported, without exception, a moment in which the standard resistance of the Dive *opened*. Not collapsed — opened. As though what a Redactor normally pushes against was briefly *not there*. The accounts vary in duration, in intensity, and in aftermath. They do not vary in the core phenomenology: something that is always present is briefly absent, and the absence is experienced not as loss but as *revelation of a structure that the presence was hiding.*

The Index Theorem has no category for this. The Theorem models resistance as the inherent property of Page-state configurations pushing back against non-default routing. Resistance is not something that *goes away*.

And yet.

If *Sôðcwide* is operating here — if the principle carried in this Instrument is in some sense active, even sealed — then what we are documenting is not the weapon removing resistance. We are documenting the weapon briefly showing users the absence *beneath* resistance. Showing them what resistance is covering.

What is beneath resistance, in the Theorem's own terms, is the structural substrate's baseline state: the condition in which nothing has been expressed yet. In which all configurations are equally unrealised.

The inscription does not say *nothingness destroys*. It says *nothingness is*. As much as everything.

I do not have a conclusion. I have a notation: the Instrument is not named after absence. It is named after the condition that makes presence possible. And every practitioner who has come within its influence and entered the Dive has, for a moment, *stood in that condition*.

I do not know what it means to stand there and come back. I know that no one who has stood there and come back has been entirely unchanged.

*— Vel'kara, Stylus. Filed 1104.*
