Ec’lesia Wonderwall
Born in 1105 to Gal’ahad Kjackter and Al’yana Wonderwall, Ec’lesia has lived a life of isolation and self-imposed darkness. Although her lineage marks her as a potential Redacteur, she avoids attention, driven by the fear of what truly resides within her. During her childhood, strange nightmares began to plague her—visions of the stars whispering incomprehensible messages, communicating something beyond human understanding. These experiences marked the beginning of her lifelong fear of the night sky and light itself.
What Ec’lesia does not know is that these nightmares were not dreams at all, but the manifestation of an ancient entity, Ai’syle, residing within her. This cosmological being, bound to light, exists beyond reality, using Ec’lesia as a vessel to experience the world. While Ai’syle exerts a constant, passive influence over her, the moon, another ancient cosmic force, shields her from its full power.
To avoid the overwhelming presence of light, Ec’lesia isolates herself in her family’s vast estate, staying in darkened rooms to keep her anxiety at bay. Her skin has become pale, her hair and clothing a deep, unnatural black, reflecting her desire to blend into the shadows. Light haunts her wherever she goes—whether she’s outside or even indoors—playing tricks with her senses, distorting shadows and colors, feeding her paranoia. She avoids sunlight, and when she ventures outside, light behaves strangely, especially when she’s alone.
Ec’lesia's powers, the signs of a Redacteur, are actually a byproduct of Ai’syle’s presence. In moments of extreme need, when she must use the full extent of her abilities, her hair shifts to a pink-blue hue, and her eyes become a kaleidoscopic rainbow—a reflection of the cosmic light flowing through her. However, the more she uses these powers, the closer she comes to losing control to the entity within.
A recluse in every sense, Ec’lesia prefers to stay in the shadows, haunted by a force she can never fully escape. She is constantly searching for answers in books, yearning to understand why Ai’syle chose her, and what it means for her future. But as her paranoia deepens, she questions not just the entity inside her, but her very existence, wondering if she will ever truly escape the light that hunts her.

Ec’lesia Wonderwall - Light Manipulation Abilities
Ec’lesia’s abilities stem from her connection to Ai’syle, a cosmic entity of light. Untrained and unaware of the full extent of her powers, she relies on instinct rather than control. Her abilities include:
Light Absorption: Ec’lesia can absorb ambient light, leaving areas in darkness. She stores this light energy within herself, which she must eventually release to avoid painful buildup.
Cooling Light to a Solid State: She can cool light to near absolute zero, forming a semi-crystalline, solid substance. This solid light can be shaped into barriers or weapons, with chilling effects that weaken and paralyze. However, forming and maintaining this state requires extreme concentration and drains her physically, making it unstable.
Reality Distortion: Through her connection to Ai’syle, she can manipulate light at a quantum level, distorting perception and creating illusions. These effects are most potent when she is under stress but are difficult to control, often causing disorientation for herself.
Energy Release: Ec’lesia can convert the solid light back into energy, unleashing it as beams or flashes. This is powerful but risky, as the energy may destabilize and affect her or others nearby.
Limitations:
Her abilities are heavily influenced by her environment and emotional state. In darkness, she has limited power, while bright environments risk overwhelming her if she absorbs too much light. Her lack of training results in unpredictable outcomes, and forming solid light can harm her if she loses control, causing the material to shatter violently or her body temperature to drop. Furthermore, using her abilities amplifies Ai’syle’s influence, causing pain, confusion, and mental strain, especially as she reaches her limits. Unaware of the full nature of her connection, her attempts to suppress these powers only make them more volatile.
