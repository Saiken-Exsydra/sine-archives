> *"Dominion is easiest to praise when it is a word in liturgy. It is harder to endure when it grows hands."*
> - Mother Salienne Karth, sealed formation note, year 2074

## What He Is

Halk'en Orveth is one of the seven living Rector-Ascendants recognized in the Church's restricted doctrine as direct Seraphic vessels. Most Hallowed clergy carry angelic fragments. Orveth carries pressure from the parent Seraphim itself: **Auctoriel, Seraphim of Dominion**.

That distinction is the center of his file. He is not simply a veteran exorcist with unusually severe authority-domain manifestation. He is a stabilized mortal contact point through which Dominion can become operative in the world at strategic scale.

In Church theology, **Dominion** does not mean appetite, rulership, or simple command. It is the sacred condition by which rightful authority becomes binding: ordination, investiture, oath, sentence, lawful restraint, and the exorcistic declaration that a hostile presence has no right to remain. Orveth's manifestation expresses that principle as **hands** because Dominion, in his case, arrives not as symbol but as contact.

He remains human. The fact has been true for nearly four centuries. The fact is also watched closely.

## Presence

Orveth appears as a severe man in late middle age and has held that general appearance for over two centuries. His body has aged into the shape his fusion now preserves: old enough to be obeyed, scarred enough not to confuse strength with innocence, and durable enough to keep carrying office long after most bodies would have failed.

He is physically imposing even outside armor: tall, broad, dense through the shoulders, with the bearing of someone who learned plate before comfort. In clerical life he favors plain black cassock, iron rosary, and almost no ornament. In the field he wears blackened pre-modern plate marked across the back with seven red palm-signs from which the manifestations can emerge.

His ordinary manner is restrained, direct, and deliberate. He does not touch casually. He asks permission before laying a hand on penitents outside sacramental necessity, removes gauntlets before blessing children, and speaks softly enough that the quiet often unsettles people more than shouting would. He is kind, but not easy. He is gentle, but never permissive.

## Backstory

Halk'en Orveth was born in 1801 in Black Mere, a northern river parish with no noble standing, no recorded Redactory lineage, and nothing in its surviving ledgers to suggest that one of the Church's greatest living assets began there. His father was a ferryman and lay cantor. His mother was a parish copyist. What marked him early was not mysticism, but practical courage: pulling children from floodwater, carrying injured clergy through winter roads, and stepping toward danger before anyone had decided whether doing so was wise.

At sixteen he entered minor seminary. Formation records do not describe a prodigy. They describe a large, quiet boy with strong memory, excellent liturgical discipline, little patience for decorative theology, and a habit of understanding doctrine as obligation rather than abstraction. He excelled in funerary work, crisis care, and exorcistic preparation long before anyone suspected direct Seraphic contact.

The turning point came in **1823**, during the **Mornhallow possession riots**. A demonic pressure event moved through a hospital by using grief-stricken patients and attendants as a distributed vessel network. When the ward became contested and experienced clergy could not enter without immediate mimicry and escalation, the newly ordained Orveth walked to the doors, placed his bare hand on the lintel, and declared that the place had been given to the sick and that the invader had not been granted leave. The voices stopped long enough for the ward to be entered. Eleven patients survived who likely would not have otherwise. Later review identified this as the first documented expression of the hand-manifestation.

The Obsidian Rite pulled him from ordinary parish duty within the month. He entered Rite formation in 1824 and proved less interested in power than in where power had to be placed. In **1831**, during the exorcism of a thirteen-year-old vessel named Anrek Voss, he manifested the **third hand** visibly for the first time, restraining the boy and pinning the possessing structure long enough for the Naming to land cleanly. Ecclesiastic Surgical monitoring began that year. Restricted theological inquiry followed. The name **Auctoriel** appears in Orveth's own handwriting by **1836**.

He was not elevated quickly. He refused advancement twice, arguing that his usefulness lay in direct field work rather than office. That position became untenable after the **Red Choir Event** of **1877**, when he manifested five hands at once inside a collapsed parish choir that had become a harmonic vessel network. He entered the nave alone. When the doors opened, the structure had been forced into a single answerable center. He was elevated to **Rector-Ascendant** in **1878**.

In **1881** he received **Sundersaint**, the greater artifact sword known as *the Parting Mercy*. The pairing was controversial from the start: a man who could impose authority should not, in principle, also bear an artifact of divine Severance. The Church accepted the risk because very few people could first make a possession structure answerable and then part it cleanly.

By **1906**, after the **Trial of Saint Kaldrin's Gate** and the first widely witnessed tangible manifestation of the sixth hand, the Diaconate stopped treating him as merely an exceptional Rector-Ascendant. Internally, he became one of **the Seven**: the rare Seraphic vessels through whom a parent-line of Heaven presses directly.

Across the next three centuries he became one of the Obsidian Rite's defining figures. He severed a rite-state from Halvorn Bridge in 1968 without collapsing the span. He manifested the **seventh hand** at **Vaul Mourne** in 2013 and turned a hospice-wide possession disaster into an answerable court, at enormous cost. He later helped shape modern Rite ethics by opposing forced investiture, arguing that burden placed without rightful permission is not office but seizure. By 2200 he has become both a living pillar of the Church and one of the clearest reminders that holy power can remain dangerous without becoming corrupt.

## The Sevenfold Hand

Orveth's primary manifestation is **the Sevenfold Hand**: seven expressions of Dominion, each deeper, more concrete, and more dangerous than the last. Church surgeons use **hand-count** as a shorthand for active manifestation level, but the theology is more exacting than simple escalation. One hand is not a weaker version of six. It is a different mode of authority.

### First Hand - The Unseen Hand

The first hand is usually invisible, appearing only under specialist observation as a faint red pressure near the right shoulder blade. Its domain is **Permission**. It establishes whether lawful authority has a place to stand at all.

Operationally, it helps validate jurisdiction, reject false consecration, resist hostile invitation rites, and stabilize the opening condition of an exorcism. Many witnesses never know it acted. They only discover that a room which should have resisted the Church suddenly had to answer it.

### Second Hand - The Veiled Hand

The second hand appears as a slender translucent red hand, often glimpsed in reflective surfaces or around oath-bound structures. Its domain is **Witness**.

It makes things answerable by ensuring they have been seen, heard, sworn, confessed, or named. It pressures lies during formal inquiry, preserves true names once spoken, exposes concealed bargains, and prevents possessing entities from hiding behind fractured memory or incomplete confession. Orveth uses it carefully because witness is one of the easiest holy powers to abuse.

### Third Hand - The Grasping Hand

The third hand is the first one most people clearly see: large, rough, scarred, and built for force. Its domain is **Restraint**.

This is the hand that built Orveth's battlefield reputation. It catches, pins, drags, blocks, holds doors and thresholds, restrains possessed vessels without automatically destroying them, and can literally close around a manifestation long enough for the rite to catch up. Soldiers remember this hand because it feels immediate. Demons fear higher ones more.

### Fourth Hand - The Open Hand

The fourth hand appears as a luminous open palm with ceremonial stillness. Its domain is **Investiture**.

It places burden, courage, steadiness, or temporary command-capacity upon another person. It cannot create structure where none exists, but it can let a priest bear more than they normally could for the length of a crisis, or let a civilian hold a consecrated object they would otherwise be unable to carry. It has saved lives. It has also left people injured by the memory of briefly becoming greater than they could remain.

### Fifth Hand - The Accusing Hand

The fifth hand is darker, sharper, and unmistakably judgmental. Its domain is **Judgment**.

It does not deliver final sentence. It identifies the point that must answer: the unlawful claimant, the hidden ownership, the false category, the part of the room or oath or body that has become morally decisive. In exorcistic terms, it reveals where the cut should land and under what truth the hostile thing must respond.

### Sixth Hand - The Hand of Mandate

The sixth hand is fully tangible. Instruments register it as structurally present, mass-like, and disruptive to ordinary readings. Its domain is **Command**.

At this depth, Orveth can impose binding instruction provided he possesses lawful authority to do so. He cannot command impossibility or rewrite moral truth. He cannot make the innocent guilty or the dead live. He can, however, halt a violent vessel, force a possessing entity to release a threshold or a voice, compel a contested structure to answer the command it has been evading, and override lesser fragment pressure in direct contest. This is the level at which the Keepers of Before classify him as a strategic instrument.

### Seventh Hand - The Sovereign Palm

The seventh hand has appeared in confirmed record only twelve times. It is not the largest; it is the most final. It appears behind him, deep red and fully formed, visible even in darkness.

Its domain is **Dominion itself**. Not permission, witness, restraint, investiture, judgment, or command, but the deeper state in which the target is placed under authority full stop. Under the Sovereign Palm, disputes of jurisdiction collapse. A possession structure that would otherwise evade language is made answerable as something that can be addressed and, if necessary, severed.

This is the most feared expression in his file. The concern is not demonic corruption in any ordinary sense. The concern is that a mortal vessel allowed to express Dominion at that depth may one day stop experiencing his own restraint as distinct from Auctoriel's will.

## Sundersaint

Orveth's artifact sword, **Sundersaint, the Parting Mercy**, is not a manifestation of Dominion. It is an older divine weapon aligned to **Severance**. The relationship is therefore not one of simple extension but of controlled cooperation between two principles: Orveth's authority makes a thing answerable; Sundersaint parts what is coherently ready to be separated.

The blade is BrightCrystal-derived, milky-translucent rather than radiant, threaded internally with red-gold veining. Its edge is less a sharpened metal line than a maintained boundary condition within the crystal. Conceptually, the sword pushes toward division along existing fault-lines. It can weaken demonic attachment to a vessel, sever false oaths, cut parasitic covenants, separate a manifestation from its borrowed medium, or divide a rite-state from the object sustaining it.

Its limits matter. Sundersaint cannot erase a demon, destroy an angelic fragment, or replace exorcism as such. It opens the place where expulsion becomes possible. It does not perform contrition, confession, Naming, or restoration. Orveth is severe on this point because people too easily confuse a clean cut with mercy.

## Character and Risk

Orveth's greatest stabilizing trait is that he does not treat power as self-expression. He is sacramentally disciplined, low in vanity, open to correction in technical matters, and still rooted in the memory of ordinary parish life. He knows Dominion is not the whole of justice. He says so often.

His danger comes from the same place as his usefulness. He has deep contempt for authority abandoned out of comfort, little patience for institutional hesitation when lives are actively at stake, and a terrible ability to place burdens on others when he judges that they can bear them. He can be enormously tender with the weak. He is much less gentle with the capable who want weakness to excuse them from obligation.

His theology can be reduced to a single line: **authority is not the right to command, but the obligation to become answerable for what command does**. That belief has saved lives for centuries. It has also made him relentless.

## Present Standing

By 2200, Halk'en Orveth is one of the Church's most powerful living assets and one of the few people alive who can resolve authority-resistant possession structures without catastrophic civilian loss. He is faithful, disciplined, pastorally stable by current institutional standards, and still regarded as necessary.

He is also what happens when Heaven's hand remains in the world long enough to learn the weight of institutions and keep closing anyway.
