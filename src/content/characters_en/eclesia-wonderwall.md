## What Changed

Older files described Ec'lesia's condition as a difficult coexistence with an external presence the Wonderwall line privately calls the Remainder. That account remains partly true. It is no longer sufficient.

Under the newer Shores framework, Ec'lesia's instability is not best understood as noise, overload, or simple attachment. The current private reading is harsher and more precise: the Remainder appears to be applying directed sink-pressure through her Light Anchor, attempting to force a Sinking event severe enough to reach the **Luminant Shore**.

That revision changes the whole file. Her problem is not only that something speaks to her. It is that something may be trying to bring her somewhere.

## Family and House

Ec'lesia belongs to a cadet Wonderwall branch that diverged from the primary line roughly two centuries ago and settled into an outwardly ordinary life in Kalt'sen. The branch retained the name, a little social gravity, and the long quiet accumulation of a bloodline inheritance nobody inside the household fully understood.

Her father, Gal'ahad Kjackter, was a non-Redactor civil engineer who died in 2189 during the Krel Tunnel collapse. His death remains the largest emotional fact of her life. Her mother, Al'yana Wonderwall-Kjackter, is a practicing Redactor of moderate standing who understands that her daughter is unusual without possessing the framework to measure how unusual. Her younger sister, El'ytra, carries the family echo in the ordinary inert form most descendants do.

The important hidden fact is dynastic, not domestic: House Wonderwall's Seat has been monitoring this branch for years because it carries the same long bloodline architecture that produced the line's rarest Light cases. Ec'lesia is the point where that inheritance converged with unusual density.

## Redactory Profile

### Radiance and Inkless

Ec'lesia is publicly classified as a **Radiance-class** Redactor and institutionally marked **Inkless**. The first label is accurate. The second says more about the instruments than about her.

Her Anchor is not absent. It is **Light** in the same deep Wonderwall sense seen only in the line's most consequential cases. The Apocachynthion's standard Anchor models failed because they were never built to read a practitioner whose Light signature is already entangled with a second structural pressure.

### Known expression

Her most documented expressions remain:

- involuntary light absorption, often under stress;
- brief coherent solidification of light into load-bearing structures;
- propagation distortion at high exertion, where distance and edges stop behaving normally;
- uncontrolled discharge, either as sleep-state flare or concentrated beam.

These effects are real, dangerous, and still not the whole story. They are the public face of a deeper contest happening underneath ordinary Dive use.

## The Pale and the Remainder

Since childhood, Ec'lesia has reported an intrusive presence associated with extreme light conditions and high-exertion Redactory output. She describes examination, fragmented communication, and a pull toward something she cannot name. Her notebooks reached an important conclusion without access to any restricted vocabulary: what she experiences is external, intentional, and trying to make itself understood.

That conclusion is correct.

The Wonderwall line calls the presence **the Remainder**: a coherence native to ungoverned gap-space, not divine in the Church's sense and not a fragment in the usual Divination sense. It recognized Em'manuel first through his Interval Anchor. Across generations, its echo propagated through the bloodline until the right architecture appeared again in a Light-bearer able to receive it.

Ec'lesia is one such bearer. The Remainder in her is not behaving like simple possession and not, so far, like active consumption. It is threading itself through her architecture slowly, persistently, and with direction.

## Revised Shore Assessment

This is the crucial update.

The older model treated Ec'lesia's case as progressive entanglement. The newer model reads it as **attempted Shorefall**.

Each meaningful act of Redactory now appears to carry a secondary risk: her ordinary Dive may be converted sideways into involuntary descent. What she records as pull, threshold-pressure, destination-sense, and the feeling of being led all fit the Shores doctrine better than the earlier attachment model did.

The likely destination is the **Luminant Shore** - the conceptual borderland of Light, revelation, exposure, and recognition.

No one knows exactly why the Remainder wants her there. The ugliest working possibilities are:

- contact with the Tidebound continuity of A'licia Rae Wonderwall;
- access to whatever survives of the First Radiance at that Shore;
- passage into a Light-borderland interval-space alone cannot fully penetrate.

Ec'lesia herself does not know any of this. What she knows is behavioral truth: she develops more slowly on purpose because deeper practice makes the presence louder. That caution is not cowardice. It is the correct response to a problem she has almost solved without being told its name.

## Academy Record

At seventeen, Ec'lesia entered a preliminary Lon'drina program after routine monitoring flagged her unregistered coherence signature. The result was immediate institutional confusion.

Three supervised sessions were attempted. Each produced excessive Depth, prolonged recovery, and the repeated detection of a second coherence structure not attributable to the subject or the room. The Academy issued provisional Quill certification and the Inkless designation mainly because leaving her fully unranked was judged more dangerous than recognizing her under suspended terms.

The specialist review meant to follow never happened. The assigned Stylus died in 2197. The vacancy remained unfilled. Her file stayed open. So did the danger.

The sealed annex matters more than the public record: the Apocachynthion authorized lethal termination if the co-presence is judged to have reached autonomous manifestation. Under the Shores model, that threshold is both understandable and alarmingly incomplete. A Shore event could be misread as something else until it is too late.

## Wonderwall Relevance

Ec'lesia matters to the Wonderwall line because she stands opposite E'lla Wonderwall in the family's private crisis architecture.

E'lla, the final product of Project Kaleidoscope, carries the cleanest reconstructed Light profile the family has ever built. The Remainder does not recognize her. Ec'lesia, the cadet-branch girl nobody built and nobody prepared properly, carries exactly the hereditary architecture the Remainder does recognize.

Project Kaleidoscope rebuilt Light.

Ec'lesia inherited selection.

That contrast now defines the private succession problem behind House Wonderwall's surface calm.

## Present Life

Ec'lesia lives in a darkened upper wing arranged around survival rather than style. She dresses in black because black is practical. She uses indirect lamps because direct light is destabilizing. She carries tinted lenses outside because the world is brighter than she can comfortably allow it to be.

She reads constantly. She writes with technical precision. She studies herself as a structural problem because nobody else has done the work for her. Her notebooks show:

- awareness that lunar position modulates the contact;
- awareness that stronger development increases pressure;
- awareness that the thing reaching for her has purpose;
- refusal to step blindly into that purpose.

She is not fragile. She is not safe. She is the most accurate analyst of her own condition currently available to any institution involved in it.

## Character

Ec'lesia's intelligence is best described as **accurate**. She reaches correct conclusions faster than better-resourced people because she refuses decorative theories and keeps only what survives contact with her actual experience.

Her grief is old, real, and not theatrical. Her social reserve is practical before it is emotional. She can be warm, but not for long in environments that demand too much performance. She is more substantial than her file first suggests and more endangered than most of the people reading that file understand.

If the Shores reading is correct, she is not merely waiting to understand what she carries.

She is racing a destination.
