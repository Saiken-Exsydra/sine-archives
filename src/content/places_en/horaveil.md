## The Industrial Capital

Hora'veil is the largest city in the Southern Region and the primary military-industrial hub of the Long War effort. Unlike Kalt'sen and Hal'vorn, it was not built for beauty or permanence — it was built for output.

The city sprawls across the southern plateau in a **planned industrial grid**, its factories and refineries consuming the BrightCrystal supply from the mining belts to the south and converting it into the energy systems, weapons, and materiel required by the frontier legions. Everything about Hora'veil is organized around production. The city's layout is functional before it is anything else, and what it functions as is the engine room of a seven-century war.

The architecture is functional and dense. Little open space exists outside designated parade grounds and the administrative quarter near the rail terminal. The permanent smell of refined BrightCrystal — a sharp, faintly mineral odor — permeates the industrial districts. The air carries grit. The sound carries the rhythm of the foundries.

Population: approximately **2 to 3 million**.

---

## The War Economy

Hora'veil's population includes a substantial proportion of active-duty soldiers rotating between front deployment and city-based rest cycles, along with the largest concentration of **Redactor military personnel** in the Empire outside of Kalt'sen. The city is simultaneously a factory, a garrison, and a logistics hub — the point where BrightCrystal from the extraction belts becomes the equipment that the frontier legions carry.

The **Southern Supply Corridor** — a dedicated military freight line running from Cal'thas south through Hora'veil to the frontier supply depots at Sarn Pell — is entirely state-controlled and not accessible to civilian freight. The underground sections of this line branch from the primary corridor at several points and extend into the defended zone beyond Sarn Pell, forming the concealed rail network that enables the Empire's mobile defense doctrine on the Ardeatus frontier.

## The City and Its Character

Hora'veil is not a city that visitors from the Central Region find comfortable. The climate is warmer and drier than Kalt'sen's persistent rain — the southern plateau's heat and dust replace the north's cold and damp. The social texture is shaped by the war: a population that rotates between production shifts and deployment cycles, a culture where military rank and industrial output are the primary measures of contribution, and a directness of manner that central citizens sometimes find abrupt.

The city does not resent this characterization. Hora'veil knows what it is for. The Empire's ability to sustain the Long War — to replace lost equipment, to field new formations, to maintain the frontier's material needs across centuries of continuous conflict — depends on what Hora'veil produces. The city takes this seriously in the way that a city built for a purpose takes its purpose seriously: without apology and without pretending the purpose is something other than what it is.
