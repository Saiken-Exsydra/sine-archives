## What HourGlass Is

HourGlass is the **SiNE Integrated Forces** — the military arm of SiNE, designated S-IF. It is organized into four commands covering land, air, naval, and orbital operations, plus the **Cloister of Applied Structure**, which handles the organization's Redactor-specialist capabilities.

It is not a private security firm in any sense that term usually implies. It is a full-spectrum military force with treaty standing, operational history at the highest-intensity theatres the Empire has faced, and an institutional depth that reflects twelve hundred years of continuous operation. The distinction between HourGlass and a state military is primarily formal — a matter of founding authority and legal structure rather than capability or operational scope.

## The Four Commands

### Threshold Command — Land Operations

Threshold Command is HourGlass's ground force. It conducts all land-based operations, from conventional military engagements to specialized operations in support of Apocachynthion or Church field work. Threshold Command maintains the largest standing personnel count of the four commands and has the most extensive operational record at the northern frontier.

Standard Threshold Command doctrine integrates conventional infantry and armored formations with Cloister-attached Redactor units and, in frontier operations, Church Obsidian Rite elements. The integration protocols were developed over generations of working alongside both institutions and have been refined to a point where they mostly run without active management.

In the War of the Threshold's ground theatre, Threshold Command is typically the sustained presence element — holding the line while specialized elements address specific threats.

### Corridor Command — Aerial Operations

Corridor Command operates HourGlass's aerial capabilities — tactical and strategic air assets, rapid deployment of Redactor specialists to engagements where ground approach is not viable, and reconnaissance operations across the frontier zone and interior.

The name reflects a doctrine as much as a mission type: Corridor Command is responsible for maintaining operational corridors — lanes of movement and communication that connect HourGlass's dispersed elements. In the Twilight Band's geography, the ability to move personnel and information quickly across significant distances is a strategic requirement, and Corridor Command's existence is partly organized around that requirement.

### Margin Command — Naval and Cold Fleet

Margin Command handles HourGlass's naval operations, with particular emphasis on the **Cold Fleet** — the maritime force operating in the northern waters of the Darkmargin.

The Cold Fleet operates in permanently dark, cold, turbulent waters in the zone where the Twilight Band's edge meets the Dark Side. This is not standard naval operating environment. The ships — designated **WVS hulls** — are purpose-built for these conditions, with structural and resonance shielding appropriate to operating in proximity to Stase-adjacent waters and in conditions where what emerges from the dark is not always categorizable under standard maritime threat doctrine.

Margin Command's Cold Fleet role in the War of the Threshold is primarily maritime corridor control — preventing Dark Ends and SOEs from flanking northern frontier positions by sea, maintaining supply and reinforcement lanes to the most isolated frontier installations, and conducting the occasional engagement that requires a platform that is simultaneously far from shore and difficult to identify.

### Glass Command — Orbital Operations

Glass Command is HourGlass's orbital arm — the least discussed of the four commands in public documentation and the one whose capabilities are most incompletely understood by external observers.

**GlassWatchers** are Glass Command's observation assets — platforms and operators responsible for the high-altitude and orbital surveillance functions that give HourGlass strategic visibility across the Twilight Band and, in the directions that matter, toward Valian-2 and the outer system.

**GlassThreaders** are the operations element — personnel and assets capable of operating in the orbital environment for functions that surveillance alone does not cover. What those functions include is not fully documented in any public record.

Glass Command's primary ground facility is in Kalt'sen. Most of its operations take place well above it.

## The Cloister of Applied Structure

The **Cloister of Applied Structure** is HourGlass's Redactor specialist corps — the body of practitioners whose training, certification, and operational deployment are managed through HourGlass's internal systems rather than through the Apocachynthion's standard framework.

This is a source of ongoing jurisdictional complexity. Practitioners in the Cloister are registered with the Apocachynthion — they have to be, legal requirements for operational deployment demand it — but their operational training and immediate command authority sit within HourGlass. The Apocachynthion's oversight of Cloister practitioners is formal where it is most visible and considerably thinner at the operational edge.

The Cloister's doctrine emphasizes integration — Redactory as a component of combined-arms operations rather than a specialist function employed separately. A Cloister practitioner is expected to understand the tactical situation their ability is being applied to, not simply to execute ability on command. This requires a specific kind of training that the Apocachynthion's academies do not provide, because the Apocachynthion's academies are not training soldiers.

## Rank Structure

HourGlass uses a distinct rank structure that does not map directly onto either the Imperial military hierarchy or the Apocachynthion's practitioner certification levels.

The senior operational rank is **Marshal-Delegate** — the designation for the commanding officer of each command and for senior figures in the Cloister. The Marshal-Delegate designation carries authority both within HourGlass and, in contexts governed by treaty arrangements, in joint operations with Imperial forces.

Below Marshal-Delegate, the rank structure follows a format that reflects HourGlass's operational history: titles that correspond to function rather than hierarchy, with seniority indicated by operational record and authorization level rather than a simple ladder. This is not confusion. It is the organizational culture of a military that has been operating long enough that the people who actually understand the rank structure do not need it labeled.

## Operational Profile

HourGlass's defining operational characteristic is **sustained presence at high-intensity theatres**. It does not deploy for a campaign and withdraw. It maintains the Cold Fleet in the north permanently. It maintains Cloister attachments in frontier installations year-round. Its Corridor Command assets are not in reserve — they are running.

This reflects both the institutional choice of an organization whose founder has been watching the world's condition for a very long time and the practical reality that the threats HourGlass exists to address do not take operational pauses.

The interaction between HourGlass's institutional culture of sustained presence and the Empire's periodic political cycles — in which governments change, threat assessments shift, and military commitments are renegotiated — is a recurring source of tension. HourGlass has outlasted more of these cycles than it has been reshaped by them. This is not an accident of organizational momentum. Em'manuel Wonderwall has been making deliberate choices about what HourGlass looks like for twelve centuries, and one of those choices is that the organization does not depend on whoever currently occupies the imperial governance structures to maintain its operational posture.
