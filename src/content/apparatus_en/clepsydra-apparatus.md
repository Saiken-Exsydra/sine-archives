## Primary Correction

Clepsydra is **not** an Obscurial, Sciel's Instrument, or a portable copy of the Instrument of Truth. It is a Precursor-built service apparatus once coupled to Sciel's jurisdiction of **Determinacy**. Sciel's Instrument law-core remains seat-fast in the Lunar Crown.

## Form and Purpose

The apparatus is a white-alloy joined polearm approximately 2.4 metres long. It separates into two balanced blades. In intact service, one half recorded the state being presented and the other recorded the configuration claimed to support it. Rejoining them tested the correspondence.

Clepsydra can expose an unsupported equivalence, pass through some Redactory overlays whose routed result lacks adequate support, and quarantine a local contradiction briefly. It cannot create truth, decide morality, read minds, invent evidence, open a superior dimension, or make its operator correct.

## The Five Oaths

The Five Oaths are recurring interlocks, not stages of permanent unsealing:

1. **Presentation** — name the state actually present.
2. **Support** — identify what configuration is claimed to sustain it.
3. **Witness** — preserve an independent record of both.
4. **Proportion** — refuse only the unsupported correspondence.
5. **Release** — end quarantine when support is restored or the claim withdrawn.

## Post-Fracture Behavior

Clepsydra refuses many hands and has crossed failed custody boundaries to reach selected operators. Three explanations remain viable: damaged interlock matching, bias from Sciel's local fragment-scars, or a narrow remote refusal through the old lunar coupling. None proves that Sciel survives as a person inside the apparatus.

Its greatest misuse is **forced correspondence**: treating one operator's model as the only admissible world. This would invert Sciel's Directive and is prohibited by the Church, the Apocachynthion, and imperial compact.
