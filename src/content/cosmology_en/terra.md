> *"A world orbiting a black hole. Tidally locked. Perpetually half-dark. Somehow inhabited. The people who built it knew exactly what they were doing. The people who live on it no longer know how much of the sky was once part of the machine."*
> - Keepers of Before, restricted annotation

## The World

Terra is not a natural planet. It is a Precursor engineering project - a laboratory environment designed and stabilized by an ancient civilization that operated at scales of time and power that most current institutions lack the framework to comprehend.

Its circumference is approximately 80,000 kilometres. It orbits **Vael'Khar**, a supermassive black hole, rather than a conventional star. The light and heat that reach its surface come from the accretion disk surrounding Vael'Khar - a permanent radiant source that does not move across the sky, does not set, and does not follow the diurnal patterns most habitable worlds take for granted.

As a result, Terra is **tidally locked**. One hemisphere faces Vael'Khar permanently. The other faces away into permanent dark. Neither hemisphere is hospitable to sustained surface civilization on its own.

---

## The Twilight Band

Between the bright hemisphere and the dark lies a band roughly two thousand kilometres wide where life is possible. This is the **Twilight Band** - the ring civilization that all known history has unfolded within.

The Twilight Band is not a single climate. It varies considerably across its length and the ring's shifting orientation relative to the accretion disk, producing what researchers divide into **six distinct radiation seasons** - not driven by axial tilt or conventional day-night cycles, but by the angular relationship between Terra's orbital position around Vael'Khar and the disc's output geometry. Some seasons bring more intense radiant energy to the band's inner edge; others ease it. The variation is predictable enough for agriculture. It is not predictable enough to ignore.

The Band's inner edge - the **Terminal Zones** - experiences the heaviest disc-side radiation and hosts more arid, high-energy environments. The outer edge transitions gradually toward the cold and dark of the **Darkmargin**, where the band's organized climate gives way to polar night. The center, the **Central Twilight Corridor**, is where most of the Empire's population and infrastructure concentrates.

## The Managed Sky

Terra's habitable surface was not prepared only from below. The world was also prepared from above.

Before the Fracture, the Moon was not merely a moon. In structural terms it was the **Intact Seal**: the complete lunar threshold architecture built to protect Terra at the point where its translation boundary was thinnest. Its sky-facing layer was **Ksy'rion** - not a separate false dome, but the outward-facing expression of the Seal as experienced from the world below.

This matters because pre-Fracture civilization did not study the farther heaven directly. It studied **Ksy'rion's curated sky**: a bounded, regulated visible cosmos through which the world could be measured safely. The heavens people inherited were real as experience and lawful as environment, but they were not a neutral window onto everything beyond Terra. They were part of the protection system.

One consequence of that protection was concealment. Objects that existed beyond the safe curated field - including what current restricted cosmology identifies as the **Quiet Moon** - were not available to ordinary pre-Fracture observation. Discovery became possible only after the Seal was damaged badly enough that Ksy'rion ceased to function as a complete explanatory sky.

## Why the World Works

The Twilight Band's stability is not natural in any meaningful sense. Maintaining a habitable atmosphere on a tidally locked world in this orbital configuration required active management of factors that unaided planetary physics would not resolve favorably.

In the earliest era of the world, this management was performed directly by the Precursors and the living systems they built to govern the world. **Aesis**, the Skyphon whose Jurisdiction covered atmospheric coherence, held the conditions of the Band in function through her direct presence in them - not engineering the atmosphere from outside but inhabiting its dynamics, correcting drift before it became instability.

The Moon's complete architecture mattered here too. The Intact Seal did not merely hold the threshold. Through Ksy'rion and the broader lunar lattice, it provided the regulated sky conditions within which climate, measurement, and inherited cosmology remained mutually legible.

After the Fracture ended that era, the Precursors' remaining infrastructure took over: a distributed network of stabilization systems embedded in Terra's structure, maintaining what the Instruments once governed through active presence. These systems have operated for the entire span of recorded history and for an indeterminate period before it. The civilizations that use the world do not generally know they exist. They do not need to. The systems continue regardless.

## The Orbital Position and the Callings

Terra's orbit around Vael'Khar is not a fixed circle. It varies. When Terra's orbit brings it to **periapsis** - its closest approach to Vael'Khar - the gravitational and energetic conditions affecting the Lunar Crown intensify. This is when the **Callings** are at their strongest.

The Callings are resonance surges originating from the wounded Moon, reflecting the containment conflict ongoing within it. Periapsis conditions do not cause them - the conflict in the Crown is continuous - but they amplify them. The correlation is well-documented by the Apocachynthion's monitoring infrastructure, though its deeper explanation remains institutionally contested.

## The Dark Side

Beyond the Twilight Band, on the hemisphere that faces permanently away from Vael'Khar, lies the **Dark Side**. It is cold, permanently lightless, and in large measure unexplored in any sustained way.

The Dark Side is not simply empty. Along its boundary with the Twilight Band - the **Darkmargin** - the Empire maintains a long military campaign against things that emerge from deeper within it. What those things are, where they originate, and how the Dark Side became what it has become is a matter the Church holds in restricted theological record and the military holds in operational necessity.

The short version that most citizens know: the Dark Side is where the bad things come from, and the northern frontier is where the Empire tries to stop them.

## What the Fracture Changed

The Fracture was not only a broken-moon event. It was also a **civilizational sky-failure**.

When the Moon shattered, Terra lost the active maintenance of the Skyphons and the completeness of Ksy'rion as a sky-mask. The sky did not stop existing. It stopped returning the old answers. Measurements that once agreed no longer agreed in the same way. Astronomical inheritance became unstable. Objects and absences that the old sky had either interpreted or concealed began entering post-Fracture inquiry unevenly, frighteningly, and without a complete framework for understanding why the world itself had not already failed.

That is the atmosphere in which post-Fracture science developed: not triumph over mystery, but advancement into a system that continued functioning after several of its most important guarantees had already broken.

---

## What the World Was Built For

The Precursors built Terra as a **laboratory Page** - a world-scale environment for the study of conditions they were interested in. The civilization that grew up in the Twilight Band was not incidental to this. Biological and social complexity was part of what the Precursors were studying, in ways that current institutions are not positioned to fully appreciate.

This history does not invalidate any aspect of life on Terra. People are born, live, and die in the Twilight Band without reference to who made the world or why. The fact that the world was engineered does not make grief less real. The fact that the sky was once curated does not make the skyless terror that followed unreal either.

What it affects is how some very old, very careful observers - the Keepers of Before, certain Precursor-adjacent figures who have been watching longer than any institution has existed - think about where this particular Page is headed.
