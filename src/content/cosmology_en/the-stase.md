## What It Is

The Stase is a region on Terra's Dark Side where **normal traversal of space and time is suspended**. It is not a geographic zone in the ordinary sense. It does not have clean boundaries that can be marked on a map, because the condition that defines it is not positional but structural - a disruption in how the underlying mechanics of reality function in that area.

It was not always there. It was created.

---

## Origins: A Precursor Experiment Gone Wrong

The Stase is a Precursor experimental failure. The precise nature of the experiment is not in any record available to current institutions. What the evidence indicates is this: the Precursors conducted research in the Dark Side that involved deliberately altering how traversal mechanics functioned in a bounded region. The experiment proceeded to a point. Then it went wrong in a way they were not able to simply reverse.

The result was a zone where the mechanics of traversal remained altered permanently - not in the bounded, controlled way the experiment intended, but in an ongoing, self-sustaining way.

The Stase has been there, in roughly its current configuration, for a very long time. Possibly since before the Fracture. The Fracture's effects on the Stase - whether the threshold compromise made it worse, or simply made what was always there more accessible to what lies outside the threshold - remain debated in restricted doctrine.

---

## What Lives There

The Stase produced two categories of entity that represent distinct problems.

**Stase Residuals** - referred to colloquially as **Dark Ends** in military doctrine - are what happens when a biological organism enters the Stase zone and becomes caught in traversal suspension. The organism does not die in the way it would outside. It persists in a state that is not life in any meaningful sense but is not death either.

**Traversal-Independent Entities** - **SOEs**, in StaSis and Obsidian Rite classification - are a different problem. These are entities that do not originate from biological organisms caught in the Stase. They are native to the altered traversal environment, or they arrived through it from wherever the threshold compromise allowed access to.

The practical distinction matters operationally: northern-front doctrine is calibrated for Dark Ends as the primary threat category. SOE engagement requires different doctrine, different resources, and different authorization.

## Access and the Nearc

The Dark Side is not uniformly accessible even to institutions that operate there.

Open-surface penetration into the deeper Stase is regarded as effectively non-viable beyond limited operational bands. The more serious restricted record instead describes **conditional access through Nearc-linked corridors**: buried, unstable routes associated with the unfinished Precursor deep-strata structure called the **Nearc**.

This distinction matters for any Dark-Side page or doctrine summary. The problem is not simply that the surface is dangerous. It is that meaningful depth access becomes possible only rarely, conditionally, and usually from below rather than across open terrain. The installations tied to such access are therefore buried research or containment sites, not ordinary frontier outposts.

## The Zone of Avoidance

One of the Stase's most important modern interpretive shifts is the **Zone of Avoidance**.

The term does not name a conventional enemy territory populated by a known hostile faction. It names a condition of **observational unreadability** along the dark-side margins: regions where instrumentation, continuity tracking, field reports, and line-of-sight observation stop resolving into trustworthy picture-building.

This is why a blank return is not automatically good news. Sometimes the world is not saying "nothing is there." Sometimes it is saying "what is there is not entering observation cleanly enough to become data."

---

## The War of the Threshold

The **War of the Threshold** is the Empire's ongoing military campaign at the Darkmargin - the frontier zone where the Twilight Band's edge meets the permanent dark.

The frontier has not moved dramatically in living memory. That does not mean the situation is stable. It means the world has developed a method of maintaining an unstable problem at tolerable distance. The War of the Threshold is not a war that can be won cleanly. It is a war that can be maintained.

The Church's field operations at the northern front exist because Dark Ends and higher-order SOEs require intervention tools military doctrine alone does not provide. The Obsidian Rite's presence in the theatre is operational necessity, not ceremony.

---

## Classification and Institutional Knowledge

The Stase is not publicly named as a Precursor experimental failure in any standard institutional channel. The military has a working knowledge of what emerges from it without possessing the deeper history of why it is there.

**StaSis** maintains the most complete living catalogue of SOE activity and characteristics. The Church holds restricted theological and operational record. Clepsydra and Continuity-adjacent doctrine contribute the increasingly important lesson that unreadability is not neutral.

None of the institutions that have pieces of this picture have assembled them into a single fully public account. Whether that fragmentation is accidental or necessary depends on whom one asks.
